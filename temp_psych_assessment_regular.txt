Short Quiz: Psychological Assessment
Chapter 1 Psychological Testing and Assessment

1. Which statement best distinguishes psychological testing from psychological assessment?
  A. Testing refers only to intelligence tests, whereas assessment refers only to personality

tests.
  B. Testing is the administration of measures, whereas assessment involves gathering and

integrating data from multiple sources for evaluation.
  C. Testing is always objective, whereas assessment is always subjective.

 D. Testing is used only in schools, whereas assessment is used only in clinics.
       Answer: B. Testing is the administration of measures, whereas assessment involves
gathering and integrating data from multiple sources for evaluation.
  Rationale: Psychological testing is narrower and refers to measuring variables through tests or
procedures. Psychological assessment is broader and involves integrating data from tests,
interviews, observations, case history, and other tools to arrive at an evaluation.

2. A psychometrician administers a cognitive test, interviews the client, reviews school records,
and observes task behavior before arriving at a conclusion. This activity is best described as:

  A. scoring
  B. norming
  C. psychological assessment

 D. test standardization
       Answer: C. psychological assessment
  Rationale: Using multiple data sources and integrating them into a conclusion is characteristic
of psychological assessment rather than mere testing.

3. Which is the best definition of a psychological test?
  A. A fixed interview conducted only by psychologists
  B. A set of items designed solely to diagnose abnormal behavior
  C. A device or procedure for obtaining a sample of behavior relevant to a psychological

variable

 D. A report summarizing the examiner's impressions
       Answer: C. A device or procedure for obtaining a sample of behavior relevant to a
psychological variable
  Rationale: A psychological test is designed to obtain a sample of behavior relevant to a
variable of interest so that inferences may be made.

4. Which statement about a test item is most accurate?
  A. It is the overall interpretation of the test score.
  B. It is a specific stimulus to which the examinee responds in a scorable way.
  C. It is the normative table used for interpretation.

 D. It is the final diagnosis based on test data.
       Answer: B. It is a specific stimulus to which the examinee responds in a scorable way.
  Rationale: An item is a specific question, problem, or stimulus on a test that elicits a response
that can be scored or evaluated.

5. A school examiner says, "This test score perfectly captures the student's actual ability." Which
statement is most appropriate?

  A. Correct, because standardized tests eliminate all error.
  B. Correct, because scores always reflect true ability.
  C. Incorrect, because tests sample behavior and are subject to measurement error.

 D. Incorrect, because only interviews can measure ability accurately.
       Answer: C. Incorrect, because tests sample behavior and are subject to measurement
error.
  Rationale: Test scores are not perfect reflections of true ability. They are based on samples of
behavior and are always associated with some measurement error.

6. Which pair is most accurately matched?
  A. Trait -- temporary condition
  B. State -- enduring tendency
  C. Trait -- relatively enduring disposition

 D. State -- stable personality characteristic
       Answer: C. Trait -- relatively enduring disposition
  Rationale: Traits refer to relatively enduring tendencies or dispositions, whereas states are
more temporary conditions.

7. A student who is usually calm becomes extremely anxious during an examination. The
anxiety shown during the examination is best classified as a:

  A. trait
  B. state
  C. norm

 D. validity coefficient
       Answer: B. state
  Rationale: A temporary anxious condition during an examination is a state rather than a
longstanding trait.

8. Which tool of assessment is most appropriate when the examiner wants detailed background
information about developmental, medical, family, and educational history?

  A. Portfolio
  B. Case history data
  C. Role-play test

 D. Projective technique
       Answer: B. Case history data
  Rationale: Case history data provide background information about developmental, medical,
educational, family, and related factors relevant to the referral.

9. Which statement best describes behavioral observation as a tool of assessment?
  A. It depends entirely on self-report.
  B. It involves monitoring actions directly, often in real or structured settings.
  C. It is limited to laboratory experiments.

 D. It is identical to a written achievement test.
       Answer: B. It involves monitoring actions directly, often in real or structured settings.
  Rationale: Behavioral observation involves direct monitoring of behavior, whether naturalistic
or structured, to inform assessment.

10. A clinician asks a trainee to respond to a simulated angry client to evaluate interpersonal
skill. This is best classified as:

   A. case history method
   B. portfolio assessment
   C. role-play test

 D. free association
       Answer: C. role-play test
   Rationale: A simulated interpersonal situation designed to evaluate performance is a role-play
test.

11. Which assessment tool is best represented by a collection of the examinee's writings,
artwork, completed projects, and work samples evaluated over time?

   A. Portfolio
   B. Structured interview
   C. Behavioral checklist

 D. Rating scale
       Answer: A. Portfolio
   Rationale: A portfolio consists of collected work samples or products evaluated over time and
can be useful in educational and other assessment contexts.

12. Which statement best reflects the role of the interview in assessment?
   A. It has no value unless supported by a projective test.
   B. It is only useful for diagnosis, not for data gathering.
   C. It can be used to obtain relevant information, clarify referral issues, and build rapport.

 D. It is always less useful than standardized testing.
       Answer: C. It can be used to obtain relevant information, clarify referral issues, and build
rapport.
   Rationale: Interviews serve multiple assessment functions, including data gathering,
clarification of problems, and facilitation of rapport.

13. Which is the clearest example of ecological momentary assessment?
   A. Administering a personality inventory once during intake
   B. Collecting real-time mood ratings through a smartphone during daily life
   C. Reviewing past school grades

 D. Conducting one final exit interview
       Answer: B. Collecting real-time mood ratings through a smartphone during daily life
   Rationale: Ecological momentary assessment refers to in-the-moment measurement of
experiences and behavior in real-life settings, often using smartphones.

14. In assessment, a referral question is best understood as:
   A. the examinee's raw score
   B. the reason the assessment was requested and the question it is meant to answer
   C. the manual used in scoring

 D. the list of test items omitted by the examinee
       Answer: B. the reason the assessment was requested and the question it is meant to
answer
   Rationale: The referral question guides the assessment by identifying its purpose and the
decision the examiner must help address.

15. Which of the following is the best example of a referral question?
   A. "What is the test publisher's address?"
   B. "How many items does the test contain?"
   C. "Is this child able to function adequately in a general education classroom?"

 D. "What color is the test booklet?"
       Answer: C. "Is this child able to function adequately in a general education classroom?"
   Rationale: This is a concrete example of a referral question because it asks what the
assessment is supposed to help determine.

16. Which group is most accurately considered part of the assessment enterprise?
   A. Only test developers and examinees
   B. Only examiners and test publishers
   C. Test developers, test users, testtakers, and society at large

 D. Only psychologists licensed for psychotherapy
       Answer: C. Test developers, test users, testtakers, and society at large
   Rationale: The assessment enterprise includes those who develop tests, administer and
interpret them, take them, and the broader society affected by their use.

17. In which setting is psychological assessment LEAST likely to be used?
   A. School placement
   B. Clinical diagnosis
   C. Personnel selection

 D. Mechanical repair of engines
       Answer: D. Mechanical repair of engines
   Rationale: Psychological assessment is widely used in school, clinical, forensic,
organizational, and research settings, but not as a direct method for engine repair.

18. A human resources department uses cognitive tests, structured interviews, and simulations
to decide promotion potential. This primarily illustrates assessment in a:

   A. forensic setting
   B. business and organizational setting
   C. neurological ward only

 D. purely academic research setting
       Answer: B. business and organizational setting
   Rationale: Selection, promotion, and personnel decisions are core examples of assessment
in business and organizational contexts.

19. Which statement best reflects the proper relationship between test data and assessor
competence?

   A. Test data are self-explanatory and do not depend on examiner competence.
   B. The usefulness of test data is linked to the knowledge, skill, and experience of the
assessor.
   C. Competence matters only in therapy, not in assessment.

 D. Competence is irrelevant if the test is standardized.
       Answer: B. The usefulness of test data is linked to the knowledge, skill, and experience of
the assessor.
   Rationale: Assessment quality depends not only on tools but also on examiner competence
and sound professional judgment.

20. Which statement is most consistent with sound assessment practice?
   A. Use as many tests as possible regardless of referral purpose.
   B. Select tools that are relevant to the referral question and the characteristics of the

examinee.
   C. Use only one test to avoid conflicting results.

 D. Avoid collateral information because it may complicate interpretation.
       Answer: B. Select tools that are relevant to the referral question and the characteristics of
the examinee.
   Rationale: Appropriate tool selection is guided by referral purpose and client characteristics,
not by quantity alone.

21. A psychometrician administers an assessment tool in a language the client barely
understands, despite the availability of a more appropriate version. Which principle is most
clearly violated?

   A. Utility
   B. Standard error
   C. Appropriateness of assessment tools to the client

 D. Alternate-form reliability
       Answer: C. Appropriateness of assessment tools to the client
   Rationale: Philippine ethical standards require use of tools appropriate to the client's
language, competence, and relevant characteristics.

22. Which statement best explains why multiple methods are often used in assessment?
   A. Because one method alone may not adequately answer the referral question
   B. Because interviews are invalid
   C. Because test manuals prohibit single-method assessment
 D. Because all assessments require at least five tests
       Answer: A. Because one method alone may not adequately answer the referral question
   Rationale: Assessment often uses multiple methods because complex referral questions
require integrated evidence from more than one source.

23. Which source would be most authoritative for learning about the proper administration,
scoring, and interpretation of a specific test?

   A. An anonymous social media post
   B. A test manual
   C. A casual opinion from a classmate

 D. A general encyclopedia entry
       Answer: B. A test manual
   Rationale: The test manual is a primary authoritative source for administration, scoring,
interpretation, and psychometric information.

24. Which statement about remote assessment is most accurate?
   A. It is never part of contemporary assessment practice.
   B. It refers to assessment conducted when evaluator and examinee are not in physical

proximity.
   C. It is identical to informal observation.

 D. It can be used only for intelligence testing.
       Answer: B. It refers to assessment conducted when evaluator and examinee are not in
physical proximity.
   Rationale: Remote assessment involves use of tools of evaluation when evaluator and
subject are not physically together.

25. Which statement best reflects a core ethical expectation in assessment under Philippine
standards?

   A. Confidentiality may be ignored if results are interesting.
   B. Informed consent is generally required before conducting assessment, unless otherwise
allowed or mandated by law.
   C. Raw scores should routinely be posted for teachers and classmates.

 D. Test materials may be freely photocopied for convenience.
       Answer: B. Informed consent is generally required before conducting assessment, unless
otherwise allowed or mandated by law.
   Rationale: Philippine ethical standards emphasize informed consent, including for
assessment, subject to lawful exceptions.

26. A psychometrician explains to a client the purpose of the assessment, probable uses of the
results, and limits of confidentiality before proceeding. This primarily demonstrates:

   A. informed consent
   B. criterion validity
   C. norm development
   D. test bias
  Answer: A. informed consent

   Rationale: Explaining purpose, uses, and confidentiality limits before assessment is a core
component of informed consent.

27. Under Philippine ethical standards, which is most appropriate regarding release of
assessment results?

   A. Results may be released to anyone who verbally requests them.
   B. Results should be released only to agreed-upon authorized parties, with proper permission
when required.
   C. Raw data should always be posted in the institution's bulletin board.

 D. Test interpretations should never be explained to the client.
       Answer: B. Results should be released only to agreed-upon authorized parties, with
proper permission when required.
   Rationale: Philippine ethical standards require that assessment data and results be released
only to authorized recipients and under proper conditions.

28. A practitioner bases conclusions on obsolete tests and outdated results despite major
changes in the client's condition. This practice is:

   A. acceptable if the report is brief
   B. acceptable if requested by a relative
   C. unethical because conclusions should not rely on obsolete tools or outdated results

 D. required to preserve consistency
       Answer: C. unethical because conclusions should not rely on obsolete tools or outdated
results
   Rationale: Ethical practice prohibits basing conclusions on obsolete tests or outdated results
that no longer support sound interpretation.

29. Which statement is most consistent with the boundary of competence principle?
   A. A practitioner may assess any condition if willing to guess carefully.
   B. A practitioner should work only within areas supported by education, training, supervised

experience, or proper consultation.
   C. Competence is determined solely by years of service.

 D. Registration alone automatically qualifies a person for all specialties.
       Answer: B. A practitioner should work only within areas supported by education, training,
supervised experience, or proper consultation.
   Rationale: Boundaries of competence are defined by appropriate education, training,
supervised experience, consultation, and related professional preparation.

30. A psychometrician is asked to perform a highly specialized forensic assessment outside
their training. The most appropriate initial response is to:

   A. proceed independently to gain experience
   B. refuse all future assessment work
   C. seek referral, supervision, consultation, or additional competence before accepting the
task
 D. copy a prior report and adjust the name
       Answer: C. seek referral, supervision, consultation, or additional competence before
accepting the task
   Rationale: When asked to work beyond competence, the ethical response is referral,
consultation, supervision, or training before proceeding.

31. Which statement best reflects professional responsibility under RA 10029 as referenced in
Philippine ethical standards?

   A. Anyone may practice psychometrics if supervised informally by a teacher.
   B. Only licensed and registered psychologists and psychometricians are legally allowed to
practice their profession.
   C. Psychological testing is outside the scope of psychometricians.

 D. Licensure is needed only for therapy, not for assessment.
       Answer: B. Only licensed and registered psychologists and psychometricians are legally
allowed to practice their profession.
   Rationale: Philippine ethical standards explicitly reference RA 10029 in stating that only
licensed and registered psychologists and psychometricians may legally practice their
profession.

32. Which action is most appropriate when delegated assessment-related work is assigned to
another person?

   A. Delegate tasks regardless of competence to save time
   B. Delegate only responsibilities the person can perform competently, with proper supervision
as needed
   C. Delegate interpretation to any available clerk

 D. Avoid informing the client that delegation occurred
       Answer: B. Delegate only responsibilities the person can perform competently, with proper
supervision as needed
   Rationale: Delegation must be limited to tasks others can perform competently, independently
or under appropriate supervision.

33. A client was referred by a school administrator. Before beginning the assessment, the
examiner clarifies who the client is, what the assessment is for, who will receive the report, and
the limits of confidentiality. This is best viewed as:

   A. test coaching
   B. clarification of third-party service arrangements
   C. item analysis

 D. malingering detection
       Answer: B. clarification of third-party service arrangements
   Rationale: When services are requested by a third party, the examiner must clarify roles,
client identity, probable uses of information, and confidentiality limits at the outset.

34. Which of the following best reflects proper protection of confidentiality?
   A. Discussing a client's results casually with friends after work
   B. Sharing identifying details in a public lecture without consent
   C. Limiting disclosure to relevant information and appropriate persons only

 D. Releasing raw scores to all staff members for transparency
       Answer: C. Limiting disclosure to relevant information and appropriate persons only
   Rationale: Confidentiality requires restricting disclosure to relevant information and only to
persons properly concerned or authorized.

35. A psychometrician uses only original test materials and avoids photocopied forms. This
primarily protects:

   A. test security and proper professional practice
   B. examiner comfort
   C. predictive validity

 D. social desirability
       Answer: A. test security and proper professional practice
   Rationale: Using original test materials rather than photocopies helps preserve test security
and supports ethical assessment practice.

36. Which statement best reflects fair interpretation of assessment results?
   A. Interpret scores without regard to validity, reliability, or client characteristics.
   B. Consider the purpose of assessment, psychometric quality of the tool, and relevant

personal and cultural factors.
   C. Treat all low scores as evidence of disorder.

 D. Use a single score as the sole basis for all conclusions.
       Answer: B. Consider the purpose of assessment, psychometric quality of the tool, and
relevant personal and cultural factors.
   Rationale: Ethical interpretation requires attention to validity, reliability, appropriateness,
purpose, and relevant client factors such as culture and test-taking abilities.

37. Which is the best example of an application-based use of assessment in an educational
setting?

   B. Computing national tax obligations

 D. Designing a building foundation
       Answer: A. Determining placement and support needs of a student with academic

   Rationale: Educational assessment is commonly used to guide placement, support planning,

38. Which statement about assessment reports is most appropriate?

39. A psychometrician becomes aware that personal stress is impairing concentration during

   B. fabricate consistency in reports

40. Which statement best captures the broad social significance of psychological assessment?

Chapter 2 Historical, Cultural, and Legal/Ethical Considerations

1. Which statement best describes the earliest large-scale use of testing in recorded history?
  A. It was developed primarily for psychotherapy outcome evaluation
  B. It was used in ancient China to select individuals for government service
  C. It began as a method for diagnosing neurological disease

 D. It originated as a procedure for measuring personality traits
       Answer: B. It was used in ancient China to select individuals for government service
  Rationale: One of the earliest known large-scale testing systems was the Chinese civil service
examination system, used to select individuals for government positions.

2. A major historical significance of the Chinese civil service examinations was that they:
  A. eliminated all social inequality in access to positions
  B. introduced the idea that official roles could be allocated partly through merit-based testing
  C. focused mainly on motor and sensory reaction time

 D. were designed chiefly for military intelligence screening
       Answer: B. introduced the idea that official roles could be allocated partly through
merit-based testing
  Rationale: The Chinese examination system was historically important because it represented
movement toward selection by demonstrated performance rather than by birth alone.

3. Which figure is most closely associated with the first practical intelligence test for school
placement?

  A. Francis Galton
  B. Alfred Binet
  C. Charles Spearman

 D. Henry Goddard
       Answer: B. Alfred Binet
  Rationale: Alfred Binet is closely identified with the first practical intelligence test designed to
help place schoolchildren appropriately.

4. Francis Galton's contribution to psychological testing is most accurately described as an
emphasis on:

  A. unconscious motives and projective techniques
  B. sensory acuity and individual differences
  C. criterion-referenced classroom evaluation

 D. multicultural counseling competencies
       Answer: B. sensory acuity and individual differences
  Rationale: Galton emphasized individual differences and attempted to measure them through
sensory and psychophysical methods.
5. The psychophysical work of early experimental psychologists contributed to psychological
assessment chiefly by:

  A. rejecting measurement in psychology
  B. showing that psychological phenomena could be studied systematically and quantified
  C. proving that intelligence is fixed and hereditary

 D. replacing all interviews with instruments
       Answer: B. showing that psychological phenomena could be studied systematically and
quantified
  Rationale: Psychophysics supported the idea that psychological events could be measured
systematically, helping establish the scientific basis for assessment.

6. During World War I, a major reason psychological testing expanded rapidly was the need to:
  A. evaluate marital compatibility
  B. screen large numbers of military recruits efficiently
  C. diagnose all psychiatric disorders precisely

 D. replace educational records in schools
       Answer: B. screen large numbers of military recruits efficiently
  Rationale: War created practical pressure for rapid mass screening, which accelerated
psychological testing.

7. Which historical trend best characterized the twentieth century development of psychological
testing?

  A. Fewer tests were developed as interest declined
  B. Testing became restricted only to educational settings
  C. Tests expanded to cover intelligence, personality, and many applied purposes

 D. Testing was replaced entirely by qualitative assessment
       Answer: C. Tests expanded to cover intelligence, personality, and many applied purposes
  Rationale: Twentieth-century testing broadened into many domains and settings, including
education, military, clinical, and occupational use.

8. Which statement best captures a major controversy in the history of assessment involving
immigrants?

  A. All immigrant testing results were found valid across language groups
  B. Early testing sometimes ignored language and cultural differences, leading to distorted
conclusions
  C. Immigrant assessment relied exclusively on nonverbal methods

 D. Early psychologists agreed that culture never affects test scores
       Answer: B. Early testing sometimes ignored language and cultural differences, leading to
distorted conclusions
  Rationale: A major historical problem was using tests with immigrants in ways insufficiently
sensitive to language and culture, producing misleading inferences.

9. Henry Goddard's work with immigrants is historically controversial mainly because it
illustrates:
  A. the superiority of projective methods
  B. the risks of drawing sweeping conclusions from culturally inappropriate testing
  C. the elimination of bias through translation alone

 D. the success of universal norms for all populations
       Answer: B. the risks of drawing sweeping conclusions from culturally inappropriate testing
  Rationale: Goddard's immigrant studies became controversial because they illustrated how
flawed methods and cultural-linguistic mismatch can produce unjust conclusions.

10. Culture is best defined as:
   A. inherited biological traits shared by a race
   B. socially transmitted behavior patterns, beliefs, and products of work of a group
   C. a person's level of formal schooling only

 D. an individual's intelligence classification
       Answer: B. socially transmitted behavior patterns, beliefs, and products of work of a group
   Rationale: This is the most accurate definition of culture in assessment contexts.

11. A culturally sensitive assessor is most likely to recognize that culture can influence:
   A. only the examinee's language
   B. only the test administrator's scoring speed
   C. the development, administration, interpretation, and use of test results

 D. only projective tests, not objective tests
       Answer: C. the development, administration, interpretation, and use of test results
   Rationale: Culture can affect every stage of assessment, not merely language or item
comprehension.

12. Which pair best contrasts individualist and collectivist cultural orientations?
   A. Individualist: role obligation; Collectivist: autonomy
   B. Individualist: self defined in relation to group; Collectivist: self as stable independent traits
   C. Individualist: independence and personal goals; Collectivist: interdependence and group

goals

 D. Individualist: obedience to authority; Collectivist: rejection of social bonds
       Answer: C. Individualist: independence and personal goals; Collectivist: interdependence
and group goals
   Rationale: This best reflects the contrast between individualist and collectivist orientations
commonly discussed in cultural assessment.

13. In assessment, "acculturation" is most relevant because it helps explain:
   A. whether a test has face validity
   B. the extent to which a person has adapted to the culture from which test norms and

expectations are drawn
   C. whether a test is speeded or power-based

 D. whether a score should be converted to percentiles
       Answer: B. the extent to which a person has adapted to the culture from which test norms
and expectations are drawn
   Rationale: Acculturation matters because it may affect how meaningfully a test's language,
norms, and assumptions apply to an assessee.

14. A clinician evaluates a person from a collectivist background using a measure of
assertiveness developed in an individualist culture. The most appropriate concern is that:

   A. the test is automatically useless
   B. culturally shaped behavior may be misread as deficit or pathology
   C. assertiveness can never be assessed cross-culturally

 D. only projective methods are ethical in this case
       Answer: B. culturally shaped behavior may be misread as deficit or pathology
   Rationale: Behavior that is normative in one cultural framework may be wrongly interpreted as
weakness or dysfunction if assessed through another framework.

15. Which practice is most consistent with culturally informed assessment?
   A. Applying norms without questioning their relevance
   B. Ignoring case history to avoid bias
   C. Examining whether the norms, language, and assumptions of a test fit the assessee

 D. Interpreting all deviations from dominant-culture behavior as maladjustment
       Answer: C. Examining whether the norms, language, and assumptions of a test fit the
assessee
   Rationale: Culturally informed assessment requires active evaluation of test fit, including
norms, language, and construct meaning.

16. Before evaluating a client whose culture may affect the validity of assessment, the assessor
should LEAST likely:

   A. review case history data
   B. consider consultation with informed cultural sources
   C. gather relevant background information with proper authorization

 D. assume that one standardized test score is sufficient
       Answer: D. assume that one standardized test score is sufficient
   Rationale: When culture may affect validity, relying on a single score is least appropriate;
broader contextual and collateral information is needed.

17. Which statement best describes a culture-fair test?
   A. A test entirely free from any cultural influence
   B. A test designed to minimize the influence of culture on administration, content, and

interpretation
   C. A test developed only for one cultural group

 D. A test that does not require norms
       Answer: B. A test designed to minimize the influence of culture on administration, content,
and interpretation
   Rationale: A culture-fair test aims to reduce, not eliminate absolutely, cultural influence.

18. The concept of a truly "culture-free" test is best viewed as:
   A. a realistic standard already achieved by most intelligence tests
   B. an ideal rather than a fully attained reality
   C. unnecessary because culture never affects testing

 D. relevant only to personality assessment
       Answer: B. an ideal rather than a fully attained reality
   Rationale: A truly culture-free test is generally regarded as an ideal, since culture permeates
language, norms, meanings, and responses.

19. Which situation best illustrates possible overpathologizing due to cultural misunderstanding?
   A. A test administrator checks scoring rules twice
   B. A collectivist client is described as maladjusted solely because of deference and

dependence in relationships
   C. An examinee asks for item clarification

 D. A school uses multiple data sources before placement
       Answer: B. A collectivist client is described as maladjusted solely because of deference
and dependence in relationships
   Rationale: This shows how culturally normative relational patterns may be misclassified as
pathology.

20. Which question is most appropriate for a culturally sensitive assessor to ask when
interpreting scores?

   A. Was the test easy to administer?
   B. How much does the test manual cost?
   C. To what extent are the norms and interpretive assumptions appropriate for this person?

 D. Is the score numerically high enough to impress the referral source?
       Answer: C. To what extent are the norms and interpretive assumptions appropriate for this
person?
   Rationale: A central question in cultural assessment is whether the norms and assumptions
of the test meaningfully apply to the individual being assessed.

21. Which statement best distinguishes laws from ethics in assessment?
   A. Laws and ethics are identical in source and enforcement
   B. Laws are optional ideals, whereas ethics are mandatory state rules
   C. Laws are rules backed by governing authority, whereas ethics are principles of proper

conduct

 D. Ethics apply only to researchers, not practitioners
       Answer: C. Laws are rules backed by governing authority, whereas ethics are principles of
proper conduct
   Rationale: Law and ethics overlap but are not identical. Laws are enforceable rules; ethics
guide right conduct.

22. In professional practice, a code of ethics primarily helps define the:
   A. test-retest coefficient
   B. standard of care expected of practitioners
   C. minimum IQ for independent functioning

 D. exact legal penalties for all errors
       Answer: B. standard of care expected of practitioners
   Rationale: A code of ethics helps define what competent, prudent professional conduct
should look like.

23. Public concern about testing most often intensifies when:
   A. tests are used in low-stakes classroom exercises only
   B. test outcomes affect access to valued opportunities such as employment, education, or

liberty
   C. assessors consult multiple data sources

 D. norms are updated regularly
       Answer: B. test outcomes affect access to valued opportunities such as employment,
education, or liberty
   Rationale: Public concern intensifies when testing has high-stakes consequences.

24. Which is the best example of a legal issue in psychological assessment?
   A. Choosing the most elegant theoretical model
   B. Determining whether a testing practice unlawfully discriminates against a protected group
   C. Preferring one interview style over another

 D. Debating whether a trait exists conceptually
       Answer: B. Determining whether a testing practice unlawfully discriminates against a
protected group
   Rationale: This is a classic legal issue because it concerns rights, discrimination, and
compliance with governing law.

25. Which statement best reflects the profession's concern about test-user qualifications?
   A. Anyone may use psychological tests as long as they are confident
   B. Access to tests should be unrestricted because interpretation is obvious

1. Which statement best describes reliability in psychological testing?

       Answer: B. The consistency of scores obtained from a measuring procedure
  Rationale: Reliability refers to the consistency, dependability, or stability of measurement. A

2. In classical measurement terms, an observed score is best understood as:
  A. True score minus systematic bias
  B. True score plus error

  Rationale: In classical test theory, the observed score is conceptualized as the sum of the true
score and measurement error.

  A. Split-half reliability
  B. Inter-scorer reliability
  C. Test-retest reliability

       Answer: C. Test-retest reliability
  Rationale: Test-retest reliability is estimated by administering the same test to the same group
at two different points in time and correlating the scores.

  A. Time sampling
  B. Item sampling
  C. Scorer bias

       Answer: B. Item sampling
  Rationale: Alternate-forms reliability addresses the consistency of scores across two
equivalent forms, focusing on whether different item samples from the same domain yield
similar results.

  B. Internal consistency estimate

1. Which statement best describes an objective method of personality assessment?

A. It uses highly ambiguous stimuli to elicit unconscious material
B. It typically uses fixed-response items scored through standard procedures
C. It requires the examinee to construct stories from pictures
D. It is interpreted primarily through examiner intuition

 Answer: B. It typically uses fixed-response items scored through standard procedures

Rationale: Objective methods usually present fixed-response items such as true-false or
multiple-choice formats, and scoring is done according to set procedures with little scorer
judgment.

2. A major reason objective personality measures are called objective is that they:

A. eliminate all response bias from the test taker
B. measure only observable traits
C. use scoring rules that require little scorer judgment
D. are always superior to projective methods in validity

 Answer: C. use scoring rules that require little scorer judgment

Rationale: The defining basis for calling these methods objective is not the absence of all bias,
but the use of standardized scoring procedures that minimize scorer subjectivity.

3. Which pair best reflects Weiner's suggested alternative to the traditional objective-projective
dichotomy?

A. Rational and empirical
B. Structured and unstructured
C. Clinical and statistical
D. Direct and indirect

 Answer: B. Structured and unstructured

Rationale: Weiner proposed replacing objective and projective with structured and unstructured
because the older dichotomy can be misleading.

4. Compared with more structured personality measures, unstructured methods are more likely
to:

A. tap relatively conscious aspects of personality
B. depend on answer keys with fixed scoring weights
C. access material beyond immediate conscious awareness
D. eliminate situational influences on responding

 Answer: C. access material beyond immediate conscious awareness

Rationale: Unstructured or ambiguous methods are viewed as more likely to access material
outside immediate conscious awareness, whereas structured tests more often tap relatively
conscious aspects of personality.

5. A clinician asks a client to choose True or False for statements about feelings, attitudes, and
behavior. This procedure is most characteristic of:

A. a projective method
B. an objective method
C. a role-play method
D. a performance-based motor test

 Answer: B. an objective method

Rationale: True-false self-report statements are a classic example of objective personality
assessment.

6. Which statement best captures the projective hypothesis?

A. The clearer the stimulus, the more valid the interpretation
B. Ambiguous stimuli permit individuals to reveal aspects of personality in their responses
C. Only verbal responses can reflect unconscious processes
D. Test interpretation should rely exclusively on face validity

 Answer: B. Ambiguous stimuli permit individuals to reveal aspects of personality in their

responses
Rationale: The projective hypothesis holds that when people respond to ambiguous stimuli, they
may reveal important aspects of their personality dynamics.

7. The Rorschach is primarily classified as a:

A. sentence completion technique
B. checklist inventory
C. projective inkblot method
D. semantic differential scale

 Answer: C. projective inkblot method

Rationale: The Rorschach uses inkblots as ambiguous stimuli and is one of the most recognized
projective methods.

A. words to elicit the first associated response
B. pictures that the examinee turns into stories
C. geometric designs that must be copied
D. true-false statements about personality traits

 Answer: B. pictures that the examinee turns into stories

Rationale: The TAT uses ambiguous pictures and asks the examinee to make up stories, unlike
the Rorschach, which uses inkblots.

B. random responding across all cards

Rationale: A major criticism of the TAT is the weakness of standardization, especially in

1. In career assessment, the primary purpose of an interest inventory is to:
