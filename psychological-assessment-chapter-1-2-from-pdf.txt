PART 3: RATIONALE

1. Correct Answer: C

    · Rationale: Psychological assessment is defined as the gathering and integration
         of data for evaluation. Testing is the more limited process of measuring variables
         to get a sample of behavior.

    · Incorrect Choices: A and B swap the definitions. D is incorrect because both
         can be collaborative or individual.

    · High-Yield Concept: Assessment = Problem-solving/Integration; Testing =
         Measurement/Behavior Sample.

2. Correct Answer: A

    · Rationale: The process begins with a referral for assessment from a source like
         a teacher or judge, often involving a specific "referral question".

    · Incorrect Choices: B, C, and D are not the standard terms used for the initial
         inquiry.

    · High-Yield Concept: The Referral Question guides the selection of assessment
         tools.

3. Correct Answer: C
    · Rationale: In collaborative psychological assessment, the assessor and
         assessee work as "partners".

    · Incorrect Choices: A, B, and D imply a more hierarchical or distant relationship
         found in traditional models.

    · High-Yield Concept: Collaboration = Partnership.

4. Correct Answer: C

    · Rationale: Dynamic assessment follows an "evaluation > intervention >
         evaluation" model and focuses on the interactive nature of the process.

    · Incorrect Choices: A is the opposite; B and D refer to physical distance or time-
         frames.

    · High-Yield Concept: Dynamic = Interactive/Change-oriented.

5. Correct Answer: B

    · Rationale: A test is a measuring device or procedure designed to obtain a
         sample of behavior.

    · Incorrect Choices: A, C, and D are specific types of assessment tools, but "test"
         is the general term for the measuring device itself.

    · High-Yield Concept: Test = Measuring device/Sample of behavior.
