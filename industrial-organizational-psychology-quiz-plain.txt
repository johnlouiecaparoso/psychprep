Industrial/Organizational Psychology ­ Chapter 2: Job Analysis and Evaluation
1. Which statement best captures the primary purpose of job analysis in I/O psychology? A. To identify the personality of the current job incumbent B. To systematically determine the tasks, duties, responsibilities, and worker requirements of a
job C. To rank all employees according to tenure and salary D. To create compensation packages without regard to job content Answer: B. To systematically determine the tasks, duties, responsibilities, and worker
requirements of a job Rationale: Job analysis is the systematic study of a job's tasks, duties, responsibilities, and the
KSAOs needed to perform it. It serves as the foundation for personnel functions such as selection, training, appraisal, and compensation.
2. A hospital wants to redesign its nurse training program, revise hiring standards, and create more job-related performance appraisals. Which HR activity should be done first?
A. Job evaluation B. Employee counseling C. Job analysis D. Salary benchmarking Answer: C. Job analysis Rationale: Before training standards, hiring requirements, and appraisals can be made job-related, the organization must first determine what the job actually entails. Job analysis provides that foundation.
3. Which of the following is the best example of a job description? A. "Must possess good moral character and a bachelor's degree" B. "Requires high emotional stability and numerical aptitude" C. "Prepares monthly reports, coordinates schedules, responds to client inquiries, and
maintains records" D. "Belongs to salary grade 18 with external market adjustment" Answer: C. "Prepares monthly reports, coordinates schedules, responds to client inquiries,
and maintains records" Rationale: A job description details the duties, tasks, responsibilities, and activities involved in
a job. Option C describes what the employee actually does.
4. Which of the following is the best example of a job specification? A. "Monitors inventory, prepares purchase requests, and updates stock ledgers" B. "Uses spreadsheets, filing systems, and procurement forms" C. "Requires attention to detail, spreadsheet proficiency, and at least one year of clerical
experience" D. "Reports directly to the purchasing supervisor"

Answer: C. "Requires attention to detail, spreadsheet proficiency, and at least one year of clerical experience"
Rationale: A job specification identifies the qualifications, skills, abilities, and experience required to perform the job successfully.
5. A company uses job analysis results to determine the relative worth of jobs for compensation purposes. This personnel product is called:
A. Job enlargement B. Job evaluation C. Job rotation D. Job crafting Answer: B. Job evaluation Rationale: Job evaluation is the process of determining the relative value or worth of jobs so that compensation decisions can be made fairly.
6. Which product of job analysis is most directly used as the basis for appraising successful job performance?
A. Performance criteria B. Labor market survey C. Recruitment source profile D. Utility analysis Answer: A. Performance criteria Rationale: Performance criteria derived from job analysis identify the standards and behaviors used to judge successful job performance.
7. An HR officer states, "We do not need to analyze jobs because our supervisors already know what employees do." Which is the best rebuttal?
A. Supervisors are always inaccurate in judging work B. Job analysis is unnecessary when jobs are simple C. Job analysis provides a more objective and systematic foundation for personnel decisions D. Job analysis is used only for compensation, not selection or appraisal Answer: C. Job analysis provides a more objective and systematic foundation for personnel decisions Rationale: Job analysis provides structured, objective, and systematic information about jobs, which is more reliable for HR decisions than informal supervisor impressions alone.
8. Which job analysis method is generally most appropriate for a highly repetitive, observable manual job such as machine operation?
A. Observation B. Personality inventory C. Assessment center D. Biodata scoring Answer: A. Observation

Rationale: Observation is most useful when job tasks are repetitive, visible, and directly observable, as in manual machine operation.
9. What is a major limitation of observational job analysis? A. It is too useful for cognitive jobs B. It may miss mental processes and decision making C. It cannot be used with incumbents D. It automatically eliminates observer bias Answer: B. It may miss mental processes and decision making Rationale: Observation is limited because many important aspects of work, especially thinking,
judgment, and decision making, are not directly visible.
10. A job analyst actually performs part of a microassembly task to understand the fine hand-eye coordination required. This method is called:
A. Simulation testing B. Participation C. Functional job analysis D. Benchmarking Answer: B. Participation Rationale: Participation involves the analyst personally performing or trying the job to gain firsthand understanding of its demands.
11. Which source would be most helpful when an organization wants to save time by reviewing prior analyses of similar jobs already on file?
A. Existing data B. Projective testing C. Forced distribution D. Cognitive interviewing only Answer: A. Existing data Rationale: Existing data such as prior job analyses, records, and related job information can save time and provide a starting point for analyzing similar jobs.
12. A job analyst interviews current incumbents, supervisors, and outside experts to gather job information. The major strength of this approach is that it:
A. Guarantees identical responses from all sources B. Allows access to information that may not be directly observable C. Removes the need for follow-up analysis D. Is always cheaper than every other method Answer: B. Allows access to information that may not be directly observable Rationale: Interviews can capture information about judgment, reasoning, hidden duties, and job context that observation alone may miss.
13. Why are multiple job analysis methods often used together? A. Because one method is legally prohibited from standing alone

B. Because different methods provide different types of information and can improve accuracy
C. Because the law requires at least three methods D. Because incumbents are not allowed to describe their own jobs Answer: B. Because different methods provide different types of information and can improve accuracy Rationale: Different job analysis methods have different strengths, so combining them helps create a more complete and accurate understanding of the job.
14. Which job analysis technique focuses primarily on the knowledge, skills, abilities, and other characteristics (KSAOs) needed to perform a job?
A. Critical incidents technique B. Job element method C. Salary survey D. Graphic rating scale Answer: B. Job element method Rationale: The job element method is person-oriented and focuses on the KSAOs necessary for effective job performance.
15. The critical incidents technique is especially useful because it: A. Produces raw IQ scores for incumbents B. Records particularly effective and ineffective work behaviors C. Eliminates the need for SMEs D. Focuses only on compensation issues Answer: B. Records particularly effective and ineffective work behaviors Rationale: The critical incidents technique identifies examples of highly effective and
ineffective work behaviors, which helps define important job performance dimensions.
16. Which statement best describes the Position Analysis Questionnaire (PAQ)? A. It classifies workers only by salary grade B. It analyzes jobs using 187 job elements grouped into major categories C. It is designed only for executive jobs D. It focuses exclusively on personality traits Answer: B. It analyzes jobs using 187 job elements grouped into major categories Rationale: The PAQ is a structured job analysis instrument that uses 187 job elements
organized into broad categories to describe and compare jobs.
17. Which of the following is NOT one of the broad PAQ categories? A. Information input B. Mental processes C. Work output D. Genetic predisposition Answer: D. Genetic predisposition

Rationale: The PAQ categories include information input, mental processes, work output, relationships with others, job context, and other job characteristics, not genetic predisposition.
18. A strength of the PAQ compared with loosely structured interviews is that the PAQ: A. Is less standardized B. Depends entirely on interviewer charisma C. Can produce more comparable results across analysts D. Ignores job context Answer: C. Can produce more comparable results across analysts Rationale: Because the PAQ is standardized, it helps produce more consistent and
comparable job information across analysts and jobs.
19. Functional job analysis (FJA) classifies jobs in terms of worker interaction with: A. Skills, motives, and interests B. Data, people, and things C. Traits, values, and culture D. Rewards, justice, and commitment Answer: B. Data, people, and things Rationale: Functional job analysis classifies jobs according to the worker's interaction with
data, people, and things.
20. Which resource is now commonly associated with FJA and broad occupational information? A. MMPI B. O*NET C. MBTI D. CPI Answer: B. O*NET Rationale: O*NET is a major occupational information system commonly used in modern job
analysis and work classification.
21. A standardized job analysis technique would be most useful when an organization wants to compare many jobs using a common framework. Which choice best fits this need?
A. Informal chatting with incumbents B. Unstructured observation alone C. PAQ D. Gut feel of the supervisor Answer: C. PAQ Rationale: The PAQ is specifically designed to analyze many jobs in a standardized way, making cross-job comparison more consistent and systematic.
22. A job analyst is warned that incumbents may exaggerate the importance of their tasks or that analysts may overlook key duties. This warning refers to:
A. The certainty of validity B. Potential sources of inaccuracy in job analysis

C. The irrelevance of training D. The elimination of bias through interviews Answer: B. Potential sources of inaccuracy in job analysis Rationale: Job analysis can be affected by bias, exaggeration, omission, and other human errors that reduce the accuracy of the information gathered.
23. Which statement best reflects the modern view of job analysis? A. It should rigidly dictate every worker action in all jobs B. It is useful only in stable factory settings C. It should provide understanding of work while still allowing flexibility in complex, changing
jobs D. It should replace all managerial judgment Answer: C. It should provide understanding of work while still allowing flexibility in complex,

requirements while still allowing adaptation and flexibility in changing environments.
24. A company wants to know not only formal duties but also how work actually gets accomplished across changing roles. Which broader concept best fits this need?
A. Work analysis B. Test security C. Performance ranking D. Adverse impact Answer: A. Work analysis Rationale: Work analysis is broader than traditional job analysis because it examines how work is actually carried out across roles and changing situations.
25. Internal pay equity is primarily concerned with: A. Matching salaries to labor market rates outside the organization B. Ensuring fairness in pay relationships among jobs within the same organization C. Giving all employees exactly the same wage D. Increasing only executive compensation Answer: B. Ensuring fairness in pay relationships among jobs within the same organization Rationale: Internal pay equity focuses on fair compensation relationships among jobs inside
the organization based on their relative worth.
26. External pay equity is best determined through: A. Compensable factor analysis alone B. Pay comparisons with relevant outside labor markets C. Personality testing of applicants D. Performance appraisal interviews Answer: B. Pay comparisons with relevant outside labor markets Rationale: External pay equity is evaluated by comparing organizational pay rates with
prevailing compensation levels in the relevant labor market.

27. In job evaluation, compensable factors refer to: A. Illegal bases for salary discrimination B. Dimensions used to judge the relative worth of jobs C. Employee benefits unrelated to work D. Methods of punishing poor performers Answer: B. Dimensions used to judge the relative worth of jobs Rationale: Compensable factors are the job characteristics or dimensions used to assess

28. Which scenario best illustrates internal pay inequity? A. A firm pays accountants less than the average paid by competitors B. Two jobs within the same firm that have similar worth are assigned very different pay levels

29. Which scenario best illustrates external pay inequity? A. Salary grades within the company are inconsistent B. A job is paid fairly compared with other internal jobs but far below prevailing

relevant market compensation levels.
30. The idea that jobs requiring equivalent KSAOs should receive equivalent compensation is called:
A. Utility theory B. Comparable worth C. Halo effect D. BFOQ Answer: B. Comparable worth Rationale: Comparable worth is the principle that jobs demanding equivalent KSAOs or value should receive equivalent compensation.

C. It refers only to differences in worker satisfaction D. It applies only to private companies, never public organizations Answer: B. It concerns possible inequities in pay for equivalent or comparable work Rationale: The pay gap issue centers on whether people performing work of equivalent or comparable value are compensated fairly.

Restricting the use and release of test results to agreed and qualified recipients Rationale: Ethical handling of test data requires confidentiality and controlled release only to

1. Which statement best defines stress in the work setting? A. A stable personality trait marked by low frustration tolerance B. A psychological and physical reaction to demands or events perceived as taxing or
threatening C. A condition caused only by excessive workload in formal organizations
D. A negative attitude resulting solely from dissatisfaction with compensation Answer: B. A psychological and physical reaction to demands or events perceived as taxing or threatening Rationale: Stress in the work setting is best understood as a psychological and physical reaction to events or demands perceived as threatening or taxing. This definition captures both the subjective appraisal component and the strain response emphasized in occupational stress literature.
2. Which employee is most likely to experience role ambiguity? A. A worker whose duties are clear but difficult B. A worker who is asked to perform many tasks in a short period C. A worker who receives inconsistent expectations and unclear performance standards
D. A worker who dislikes a supervisor's leadership style Answer: C. A worker who receives inconsistent expectations and unclear performance standards Rationale: Role ambiguity exists when job duties, standards, or expectations are not clearly defined. Unclear performance expectations and inconsistent messages directly produce this form of stress.
3. Which situation best illustrates role conflict? A. A manager is uncertain about how performance will be rated B. A supervisor must protect subordinates while also implementing a management order that
harms the team C. An employee performs repetitive work below his skill level
D. A worker is exposed to constant machine noise Answer: B. A supervisor must protect subordinates while also implementing a management order that harms the team Rationale: Role conflict occurs when expectations tied to one role interfere with expectations tied to another. A supervisor caught between loyalty to subordinates and directives from upper management is a classic example.
4. Role overload is best described as a condition in which the employee: A. Performs routine work that does not use existing competencies B. Experiences incompatible demands from two or more roles C. Lacks enough work to remain mentally engaged

D. Perceives that available time, skill, or resources are insufficient to meet demands Answer: D. Perceives that available time, skill, or resources are insufficient to meet demands Rationale: Role overload develops when employees feel they cannot complete required tasks because of limited time, inadequate resources, or insufficient capability relative to demands.
5. Underutilization becomes a stressor when: A. Pay is below industry standards B. The worker feels that knowledge, skills, or energy are not being fully used C. The supervisor frequently gives developmental feedback
D. The employee is promoted too rapidly Answer: B. The worker feels that knowledge, skills, or energy are not being fully used Rationale: Underutilization becomes stressful when employees feel their competencies, energy, or abilities are not being used meaningfully, often in monotonous or low-demand roles.
6. Which is an example of an organizational stressor rather than an individual dispositional stressor?
A. Type A behavior pattern B. Low resilience C. Poor person­job fit caused by unclear job design
D. Pessimistic thinking style Answer: C. Poor person­job fit caused by unclear job design Rationale: Organizational stressors arise from the work environment, job design, or role structure. Poor person­job fit caused by the way work is designed is an organizational source rather than a dispositional one.
7. Which job condition is most directly associated with stress arising from lack of control? A. Strong employee autonomy in decision-making B. High discretion over pacing and methods C. Little influence over work procedures and outcomes
D. High emotional stability Answer: C. Little influence over work procedures and outcomes Rationale: A lack of control is a known work stressor. When employees have little autonomy or influence over how their work is done, stress tends to increase.
8. Work­family conflict refers to: A. Disagreement between spouses regarding income allocation B. Stress that arises when work and family role demands compete C. Conflict between family members employed in the same company
D. Family opposition to an employee's promotion Answer: B. Stress that arises when work and family role demands compete Rationale: Work­family conflict refers to cumulative stress that results when the demands of work and family roles compete or interfere with one another.

9. Which is the best example of a physical work environment stressor? A. Interpersonal jealousy B. Role conflict C. Noise and uncomfortable temperature
D. Lack of organizational commitment Answer: C. Noise and uncomfortable temperature Rationale: Noise and temperature are classic physical work environment stressors. They originate in the surroundings of the job rather than in personality or role structure.
10. Which employee is most vulnerable to stress-related strain based on dispositional factors? A. A Type B employee with high optimism B. A resilient worker with strong coping resources C. A pessimistic employee with Type A tendencies
D. An employee with high autonomy and low workload Answer: C. A pessimistic employee with Type A tendencies Rationale: Type A behavior pattern and pessimism are associated with greater vulnerability to stress and stress-related strain compared with Type B patterns and optimism.
11. Which method is most directly based on the employee's own perception of stress? A. Blood pressure monitoring B. Cortisol assay C. Self-report stress inventory
D. Security incident log Answer: C. Self-report stress inventory Rationale: Self-report measures directly assess perceived stress by asking employees to rate their own experience of strain, workload, ambiguity, or related symptoms.
12. A major limitation of physiological measures of stress is that they: A. Cannot be used in organizations B. Are always less valid than interviews C. Show substantial variation across persons and time and require trained personnel
D. Measure only attitudes toward the organization Answer: C. Show substantial variation across persons and time and require trained personnel Rationale: Physiological stress measures can vary considerably across persons and across time and often require medical equipment and trained personnel, making them costly and sometimes difficult to interpret.
13. Which finding is most characteristic of job burnout? A. Increased novelty seeking and risk taking B. Emotional exhaustion, depersonalization, and reduced personal accomplishment C. High morale with moderate fatigue D. Temporary boredom relieved by task rotation alone

 Answer: B. Emotional exhaustion, depersonalization, and reduced personal
accomplishment Rationale: Burnout is classically characterized by emotional exhaustion, depersonalization or
cynicism, and a reduced sense of personal accomplishment.
14. Burnout is especially common in occupations that: A. Involve little social interaction B. Require repetitive machine-paced work only C. Emphasize service, helping, or emotional labor
D. Allow total work-from-home arrangements Answer: C. Emphasize service, helping, or emotional labor Rationale: Burnout is especially common in human service and helping professions because these roles often require sustained emotional labor and intensive interpersonal demands.
15. Which outcome is most likely when chronic work stress is left unmanaged? A. Increased job satisfaction B. Lower absenteeism C. Greater organizational commitment
D. Higher turnover intentions Answer: D. Higher turnover intentions Rationale: Chronic work stress is associated with several negative outcomes, including burnout, absenteeism, lowered satisfaction, and stronger intentions to leave the organization.
16. Which is an individual coping strategy for stress reduction? A. Clarifying job roles across the department B. Redesigning jobs to improve person­job fit C. Exercise, relaxation, and cognitive restructuring
D. Revising staffing levels organization-wide Answer: C. Exercise, relaxation, and cognitive restructuring Rationale: These are individual coping strategies because they are actions taken by the employee personally to manage stress and its effects.
17. Which is an organizational coping strategy? A. Meditation practiced by a single employee B. Personal dietary modification C. Improved communication and role clarification by management
D. Private journaling after work Answer: C. Improved communication and role clarification by management Rationale: Organizational coping strategies are systems-level responses. Better communication and role clarification reduce uncertainty and situational stress across employees.
18. Cognitive restructuring is best understood as: A. Replacing trained employees with more resilient ones

B. Changing the way one interprets and thinks about stressors C. Reducing workload through automation
D. Using sanctions to stop deviant behavior Answer: B. Changing the way one interprets and thinks about stressors Rationale: Cognitive restructuring is a coping method that targets thoughts and appraisals, helping individuals reinterpret stressors in less harmful ways.
19. Which action is most likely to reduce stress caused by job uncertainty? A. Keeping performance criteria informal B. Providing clear feedback and clarifying duties C. Increasing task ambiguity to promote flexibility
D. Rotating supervisors every week Answer: B. Providing clear feedback and clarifying duties Rationale: Job uncertainty and ambiguity are reduced when supervisors clarify duties and provide regular performance feedback.
20. In the context of stress management, resilience training is intended primarily to: A. Eliminate all organizational stressors B. Increase employees' capacity to cope effectively with demands C. Replace performance appraisal systems
D. Screen out applicants with low extraversion Answer: B. Increase employees' capacity to cope effectively with demands Rationale: Resilience training aims to strengthen employees' adaptive coping capacity, often through cognitive, emotional, and self-efficacy-related development.
21. Which statement about workplace bullying is most accurate? A. It is limited to physical assault B. It includes repeated hostile behaviors such as intimidation, ridicule, and social isolation C. It is harmless unless it affects compensation
