MOCK EXAM I/O PSYCHOLOGY
EXAM 1
MOCK EXAM I/O PSYCHOLOGY EXAM 1
1. Industrial/Organizational psychology is best defined as the field that:
A. Studies mental disorders in workplace settings
B. Applies psychological principles to understand and improve behavior at work
C. Focuses only on personnel selection and testing
D. Deals primarily with labor law and compensation disputes
✅Answer: B. Applies psychological principles to understand and improve behavior at work
Rationale: I/O psychology studies behavior in work settings and applies psychological
principles to improve work outcomes, employee functioning, and organizational effectiveness.
2. Which activity is most clearly within the traditional industrial side of I/O psychology?
A. Studying group cohesion in project teams
B. Designing a selection battery for call center applicants
C. Examining organizational culture
D. Analyzing political behavior in organizations
✅Answer: B. Designing a selection battery for call center applicants
Rationale: The industrial side focuses on staffing, selection, training, and performance
measurement. Designing a selection battery fits this area directly.
3. Which statement best reflects the scientist-practitioner model in I/O psychology?
A. Practice is more important than research because work settings change quickly
B. Research and practice should inform one another in solving workplace problems
C. Scientific work should be limited to universities
D. Practitioners should avoid theory because it complicates decisions
✅Answer: B. Research and practice should inform one another in solving workplace
problems
Rationale: The scientist-practitioner model holds that sound practice should be grounded in
research and research should address real workplace problems.
4. Which early contributor is most strongly associated with scientific management?
A. Hugo Munsterberg
B. Walter Dill Scott
C. Frederick W. Taylor
D. Elton Mayo
✅Answer: C. Frederick W. Taylor
Rationale: Taylor is associated with scientific management and time-and-motion studies aimed
at increasing efficiency.
5. A company increases productivity after changing lighting levels, but productivity also
increases when lighting is later reduced. This finding is most closely associated with:
A. Goal-setting theory
B. The Hawthorne effect
C. Equity theory
D. Expectancy theory
✅Answer: B. The Hawthorne effect
Rationale: The Hawthorne effect refers to behavior change resulting from attention or
awareness of being studied, not necessarily from the work condition itself.
6. Which historical development most directly increased the emphasis on fairness in employee
selection?
A. Human relations movement
B. Civil rights legislation
C. Time-and-motion studies
D. Trait theory of leadership
✅Answer: B. Civil rights legislation
Rationale: Civil rights laws sharply increased attention to fair, job-related, and legally
defensible selection systems.
7. In a validation study, an organization finds that a cognitive ability test predicts training
success. This evidence primarily supports the test’s:
A. Reliability
B. Content validity
C. Criterion-related validity
D. Face validity
✅Answer: C. Criterion-related validity
Rationale: If a test predicts an important outcome such as training success, that is
criterion-related validity.
8. Which research design provides the strongest basis for causal inference?
A. Case study
B. Correlational study
C. Experimental design
D. Archival survey
✅Answer: C. Experimental design
Rationale: Because experiments involve manipulation and control, they provide the strongest
basis for causal claims.
9. An I/O psychologist examines the relationship between job satisfaction and absenteeism
without manipulating either variable. This is an example of:
A. Experimental research
B. Correlational research
C. Meta-analysis
D. Action research
✅Answer: B. Correlational research
Rationale: The researcher is measuring naturally occurring variables and examining their
relationship without manipulation.
10. A researcher combines results from many studies on the relationship between structured
interviews and job performance. This is:
A. Test-retest design
B. Quasi-experiment
C. Meta-analysis
D. Cross-validation
✅Answer: C. Meta-analysis
Rationale: Meta-analysis statistically summarizes findings across multiple studies.
11. Which statement about reliability is most accurate?
A. A test can be valid even if it is completely unreliable
B. Reliability refers to consistency of measurement
C. Reliability guarantees fairness
D. Reliability and validity are the same concept
✅Answer: B. Reliability refers to consistency of measurement
Rationale: Reliability concerns whether a measure yields stable and consistent results.
12. When a test appears job-related to applicants, it is high in:
A. Predictive validity
B. Internal consistency
C. Face validity
D. Differential validity
✅Answer: C. Face validity
Rationale: Face validity reflects whether a test appears relevant and acceptable to test
takers.
13. Which method would be most appropriate for identifying actual tasks performed by a
machine operator during a shift?
A. Personality inventory
B. Direct observation
C. Assessment center
D. Organizational survey
✅Answer: B. Direct observation
Rationale: Direct observation is especially useful for visible, concrete job tasks such as
machine operation.
14. A major purpose of job analysis is to:
A. Eliminate the need for performance appraisal
B. Identify job duties and worker requirements
C. Predict national labor trends
D. Replace training needs assessment
✅Answer: B. Identify job duties and worker requirements
Rationale: Job analysis defines tasks, duties, responsibilities, and the KSAOs needed for
performance.
15. Which job analysis method relies heavily on observing critical behaviors that distinguish
effective from ineffective performance?
A. Critical incidents technique
B. Assessment center
C. Forced distribution
D. Graphic rating scale
✅Answer: A. Critical incidents technique
Rationale: This method collects examples of effective and ineffective behaviors to identify
important job requirements.
16. O*NET is most useful because it:
A. Serves as a national database of occupational information
B. Replaces local job analysis in all cases
C. Measures employee motivation directly
D. Eliminates legal concerns in selection
✅Answer: A. Serves as a national database of occupational information
Rationale: O*NET provides standardized occupational descriptions and job-related
information across occupations.
17. In recruitment, the primary purpose of a realistic job preview is to:
A. Attract as many applicants as possible regardless of fit
B. Increase initial applicant reactions only
C. Reduce turnover by providing balanced job information
D. Replace structured interviews
✅Answer: C. Reduce turnover by providing balanced job information
Rationale: Realistic job previews help applicants form accurate expectations, reducing later
dissatisfaction and turnover.
18. Which recruitment source is most likely to increase workforce homogeneity if overused?
A. Public employment office
B. Employee referrals
C. Online job boards
D. University placement centers
✅Answer: B. Employee referrals
Rationale: Referral systems may reproduce existing workforce similarity if employees
recommend people like themselves.
19. A predictor shows adverse impact against a protected group. The employer’s best defense
is to show that the predictor:
A. Is popular among supervisors
B. Has high face validity
C. Is job related and consistent with business necessity
D. Is cheaper than alternatives
✅Answer: C. Is job related and consistent with business necessity
Rationale: Where adverse impact exists, the employer must typically demonstrate that the
procedure is job related and necessary.
20. Which statement best describes a bona fide occupational qualification?
A. Any requirement preferred by management
B. A qualification reasonably necessary to the normal operation of the business
C. Any trait related to organizational culture
D. A general defense for intelligence tests
✅Answer: B. A qualification reasonably necessary to the normal operation of the business
Rationale: A BFOQ is a narrow legal exception allowing certain otherwise discriminatory
qualifications when essential to business operation.
21. Which interview type generally produces the highest reliability and validity?
A. Unstructured interview
B. Stress interview
C. Structured interview
D. Panel interview without standardized scoring
✅Answer: C. Structured interview
Rationale: Structured interviews standardize questions, scoring, and evaluation criteria,
improving reliability and validity.
22. Which is the strongest advantage of structured interviews over unstructured interviews?
A. They are always shorter
B. They reduce interviewer discretion and increase standardization
C. They eliminate faking
D. They do not require job analysis
✅Answer: B. They reduce interviewer discretion and increase standardization
Rationale: This reduces bias, increases comparability across candidates, and strengthens
defensibility.
23. A situational interview question usually asks applicants:
A. To describe a past behavior
B. To interpret a personality profile
C. What they would do in a hypothetical job situation
D. To complete a work sample
✅Answer: C. What they would do in a hypothetical job situation
Rationale: Situational interviews present job-relevant hypothetical scenarios and ask how the
applicant would respond.
24. Biodata is best described as:
A. A biological assessment of stress hormones
B. Weighted information about an applicant’s life and work history
C. A clinical interview used in diagnosis
D. A method for observing behavior on the job
✅Answer: B. Weighted information about an applicant’s life and work history
Rationale: Biodata uses biographical information shown empirically to relate to work
outcomes.
25. Which selection method has especially strong content validity when it closely resembles
actual job tasks?
A. Integrity test
B. Work sample
C. Interest inventory
D. Graphology
✅Answer: B. Work sample
Rationale: Work samples are highly job related because they require applicants to perform
representative job tasks.
26. Integrity tests are primarily intended to predict:
A. Physical stamina
B. Counterproductive work behaviors
C. Cognitive complexity
D. Leadership emergence
✅Answer: B. Counterproductive work behaviors
Rationale: Integrity tests are intended to predict theft, dishonesty, rule-breaking, and related
deviance.
27. Which statement about cognitive ability tests is most accurate?
A. They are generally poor predictors of job performance
B. They often predict training and job performance well
C. They cannot be validated legally
D. They measure only acquired knowledge
✅Answer: B. They often predict training and job performance well
Rationale: Cognitive ability is one of the strongest general predictors of training success and
many forms of job performance.
28. Assessment centers are especially appropriate when selecting:
A. Entry-level workers for repetitive manual tasks only
B. Applicants for managerial or supervisory roles
C. Applicants when no job analysis exists
D. Workers for jobs requiring no interpersonal skill
✅Answer: B. Applicants for managerial or supervisory roles
Rationale: Assessment centers are commonly used for complex roles involving leadership,
communication, and decision making.
29. Which of the following best illustrates criterion contamination?
A. A predictor fails to cover all job tasks
B. A performance criterion is influenced by factors unrelated to actual performance
C. The criterion is too broad
D. Applicants dislike the test
✅Answer: B. A performance criterion is influenced by factors unrelated to actual
performance
Rationale: Criterion contamination occurs when the criterion includes irrelevant influences
such as favoritism or opportunity differences.
30. If a performance appraisal is used primarily to improve employee development, the rater
should emphasize:
A. Punitive documentation
B. Specific behavioral feedback
C. Forced ranking only
D. Seniority-based comparisons
✅Answer: B. Specific behavioral feedback
Rationale: Developmental appraisal works best when employees receive concrete,
behavior-focused feedback they can act on.
31. Which source of appraisal information is especially vulnerable to halo error?
A. Objective production records
B. Single overall supervisor impressions
C. Electronic performance logs
D. Work sample tests
✅Answer: B. Single overall supervisor impressions
Rationale: A rater relying on general impressions is especially prone to halo effects.
32. A supervisor rates an employee high on all dimensions because the employee is very
punctual. This is an example of:
A. Central tendency
B. Recency effect
C. Halo error
D. Contrast effect
✅Answer: C. Halo error
Rationale: A single favorable trait spreads across unrelated rating dimensions.
33. Recency error occurs when:
A. Raters give everyone average scores
B. Recent behavior is weighted too heavily in appraisal
C. The first impression dominates ratings
D. Supervisors compare employees with themselves
✅Answer: B. Recent behavior is weighted too heavily in appraisal
Rationale: Recency error overemphasizes what happened just before the rating.
34. Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scales are designed primarily to:
A. Eliminate the need for rater training
B. Link performance dimensions with specific behavioral examples
C. Rank employees from best to worst
D. Measure personality rather than performance
✅Answer: B. Link performance dimensions with specific behavioral examples
Rationale: BARS anchors scale points with examples of actual job behavior, improving clarity.
35. Which appraisal system is most likely to increase legal defensibility?
A. Vague traits with no documentation
B. Standardized dimensions derived from job analysis
C. Informal impressions by managers
D. Ratings based solely on loyalty
✅Answer: B. Standardized dimensions derived from job analysis
Rationale: Job-related, documented, standardized appraisal systems are more defensible
legally and more accurate.
36. A 360-degree feedback system typically includes ratings from:
A. Supervisors only
B. Peers only
C. Multiple sources such as supervisors, peers, and subordinates
D. Customers only
✅Answer: C. Multiple sources such as supervisors, peers, and subordinates
Rationale: 360-degree feedback gathers perspectives from several relevant raters.
37. The first step in effective training design is usually to:
A. Deliver the program
B. Conduct a training needs assessment
C. Reward attendance
D. Select a trainer based on popularity
✅Answer: B. Conduct a training needs assessment
Rationale: Training should begin with diagnosing whether training is needed and what type is
needed.
38. Which type of training needs analysis focuses on whether an individual employee lacks
knowledge, skill, or readiness?
A. Organizational analysis
B. Task analysis
C. Person analysis
D. Utility analysis
✅Answer: C. Person analysis
Rationale: Person analysis examines who needs training and whether the individual lacks
required competencies or readiness.
39. A company wants trainees to practice handling angry customers in a safe but realistic
format. The best method is:
A. Lecture
B. Role-play
C. Job rotation only
D. Programmed text
✅Answer: B. Role-play
Rationale: Role-play is ideal for practicing interpersonal responses in realistic but controlled
situations.
40. Transfer of training is most likely when:
A. The training content is unrelated to job demands
B. Supervisors discourage use of new skills
C. Trainees can practice behaviors similar to those required on the job
D. Training occurs without feedback
✅Answer: C. Trainees can practice behaviors similar to those required on the job
Rationale: Transfer improves when training closely resembles job demands and trainees can
apply new skills.
41. Which level of training evaluation focuses on whether trainees liked the program?
A. Reaction
B. Learning
C. Behavior
D. Results
✅Answer: A. Reaction
Rationale: Reaction evaluation assesses trainee satisfaction or immediate impressions of the
program.
42. Which statement about motivation is most consistent with expectancy theory?
A. People are motivated only by unmet physiological needs
B. Motivation depends on expected links among effort, performance, and outcomes
C. Motivation is caused mainly by reinforcement history
D. Motivation depends only on fairness judgments
✅Answer: B. Motivation depends on expected links among effort, performance, and
outcomes
Rationale: Expectancy theory proposes that motivation depends on expectancy,
instrumentality, and valence.
43. An employee works hard because she believes effort will improve performance and good
performance will lead to a valued bonus. This is best explained by:
A. Equity theory
B. Expectancy theory
C. Need hierarchy
D. Two-factor theory
✅Answer: B. Expectancy theory
Rationale: The employee believes effort leads to performance and performance leads to a
valued reward.
44. According to equity theory, employees are most likely to feel distress when they:
A. Receive feedback
B. Perceive imbalance between their inputs and outcomes relative to others
C. Have high achievement needs
D. Lack autonomy
✅Answer: B. Perceive imbalance between their inputs and outcomes relative to others
Rationale: Equity theory centers on social comparison and perceived fairness of exchange
ratios.
45. Which theory is most closely associated with the importance of specific, difficult goals?
A. Goal-setting theory
B. Drive reduction theory
C. Human relations theory
D. Trait activation theory
✅Answer: A. Goal-setting theory
Rationale: Goal-setting theory highlights the motivational effect of specific and challenging
goals.
46. In Herzberg’s two-factor theory, salary and company policy are usually considered:
A. Motivators
B. Hygiene factors
C. Growth needs
D. Valences
✅Answer: B. Hygiene factors
Rationale: In Herzberg’s theory, salary and policy prevent dissatisfaction but do not
necessarily create lasting satisfaction.
47. According to the Job Characteristics Model, experienced meaningfulness is promoted by:
A. Low autonomy and low feedback
B. Skill variety, task identity, and task significance
C. Extrinsic rewards only
D. Strict supervision and close control
✅Answer: B. Skill variety, task identity, and task significance
Rationale: These core job dimensions contribute to experienced meaningfulness in the Job
Characteristics Model.
48. Self-determination theory emphasizes that intrinsic motivation is strengthened when work
supports:
A. Centralization, surveillance, and punishment
B. Autonomy, competence, and relatedness
C. Seniority and tenure
D. Competition and scarcity
✅Answer: B. Autonomy, competence, and relatedness
Rationale: Self-determination theory emphasizes these basic psychological needs.
49. Job satisfaction is best described as:
A. A permanent personality trait
B. A positive emotional response toward one’s job
C. The same as organizational commitment
D. An objective measure of productivity
✅Answer: B. A positive emotional response toward one’s job
Rationale: Job satisfaction is an evaluative and affective response to one’s work or job
conditions.
50. Which form of commitment reflects staying with an organization because leaving would be
costly?
A. Affective commitment
B. Normative commitment
C. Continuance commitment
D. Moral commitment
✅Answer: C. Continuance commitment
Rationale: Continuance commitment involves staying because the perceived cost of leaving
is high.
51. Organizational citizenship behavior refers to:
A. Minimum required performance
B. Voluntary behavior that supports the organization but is not formally required
C. Union activity
D. Turnover intention
✅Answer: B. Voluntary behavior that supports the organization but is not formally required
Rationale: OCB includes extra-role helpful behavior that benefits the organization.
52. Which statement about job satisfaction and performance is most accurate?
A. They are completely unrelated
B. High performance always causes dissatisfaction
C. They are positively related, though the relationship is not perfect
D. Satisfaction matters only in clerical jobs
✅Answer: C. They are positively related, though the relationship is not perfect
Rationale: Research generally shows a modest positive relationship between satisfaction and
performance.
53. Which is the best example of absenteeism as a work attitude outcome?
A. Staying late to help a coworker
B. Frequently failing to report for scheduled work
C. Reporting harassment
D. Applying for promotion
✅Answer: B. Frequently failing to report for scheduled work
Rationale: Absenteeism involves failure to attend scheduled work and is often linked to
negative work attitudes.
54. Burnout is most commonly characterized by:
A. Emotional exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced accomplishment
B. Increased intrinsic motivation
C. High organizational commitment
D. Improved concentration under stress
✅Answer: A. Emotional exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced accomplishment
Rationale: These are the classic components associated with burnout.
55. Role ambiguity is present when an employee:
A. Has too many tasks
B. Receives incompatible job demands
C. Is unclear about expectations and responsibilities
D. Works long hours
✅Answer: C. Is unclear about expectations and responsibilities
Rationale: Role ambiguity occurs when employees do not know what is expected of them.
56. Role conflict occurs when:
A. A worker does not understand instructions
B. Two or more job demands are incompatible
C. A person lacks ability
D. A task is repetitive
✅Answer: B. Two or more job demands are incompatible
Rationale: Role conflict arises when fulfilling one expectation makes it difficult to fulfill another.
57. Which is an example of a dispositional source of stress?
A. Excessive workload
B. Ambiguous policies
C. Type A pattern or negative affectivity
D. Unsafe lighting
✅Answer: C. Type A pattern or negative affectivity
Rationale: Dispositional stressors come from characteristics within the individual.
58. Counterproductive work behavior includes:
A. Helping a new employee learn the job
B. Voluntary attendance at safety training
C. Theft, sabotage, or chronic withdrawal
D. Suggesting process improvements
✅Answer: C. Theft, sabotage, or chronic withdrawal
Rationale: These are classic counterproductive work behaviors.
59. Which organizational strategy is most likely to reduce stress?
A. Increasing role ambiguity
B. Removing employee control where possible
C. Clarifying roles and improving supervisor support
D. Encouraging constant overtime
✅Answer: C. Clarifying roles and improving supervisor support
Rationale: Stress is reduced when expectations are clear and support is available.
60. In communication, the grapevine refers to:
A. Formal top-down memos
B. Informal channels through which information spreads
C. A legal grievance system
D. Computerized performance dashboards
✅Answer: B. Informal channels through which information spreads
Rationale: The grapevine is the organization’s informal communication network.
61. Which communication flow occurs when employees send information to higher levels of
management?
A. Downward
B. Upward
C. Lateral
D. Diagonal only
✅Answer: B. Upward
Rationale: Upward communication moves from lower to higher organizational levels.
62. Noise in the communication process refers to:
A. Loud sound in the workplace only
B. Any factor that interferes with accurate message transmission or interpretation
C. The sender’s authority
D. Formal chain of command
✅Answer: B. Any factor that interferes with accurate message transmission or interpretation
Rationale: Noise includes physical, semantic, psychological, or organizational interference.
63. Group cohesion generally refers to:
A. The amount of conflict in a group
B. The attractiveness of the group to its members
C. The size of the formal hierarchy
D. The degree of centralization
✅Answer: B. The attractiveness of the group to its members
Rationale: Cohesion reflects the strength of members’ desire to remain in and support the
group.
64. Groupthink is most likely when:
A. Members critically evaluate alternatives
B. Cohesive groups discourage dissent and strive for unanimity
C. Roles are clearly differentiated
D. Team diversity is high and well managed
✅Answer: B. Cohesive groups discourage dissent and strive for unanimity
Rationale: Groupthink involves pressure toward consensus and insufficient critical evaluation.
65. Social loafing is best described as:
A. Better performance in the presence of others
B. Reduced individual effort when working in a group
C. Conflict between departments
D. A norm of helping behavior
✅Answer: B. Reduced individual effort when working in a group
Rationale: Social loafing occurs when individual effort declines because responsibility is
diffused.
66. Which conflict style reflects high concern for self and low concern for others?
A. Avoiding
B. Accommodating
C. Competing
D. Collaborating
✅Answer: C. Competing
Rationale: Competing reflects assertiveness for one’s own goals with relatively low
cooperativeness.
67. Which statement best distinguishes work teams from simple work groups?
A. Teams never experience conflict
B. Teams usually require interdependence and shared goals
C. Teams are always larger than groups
D. Teams eliminate individual accountability
✅Answer: B. Teams usually require interdependence and shared goals
Rationale: Teams are distinguished by coordinated effort and interdependent work toward
shared outcomes.
68. Trait theories of leadership assume that:
A. Leadership depends only on training
B. Certain individual characteristics are associated with leadership effectiveness
C. Leadership is irrelevant to performance
D. All leaders behave the same way
✅Answer: B. Certain individual characteristics are associated with leadership effectiveness
Rationale: Trait approaches focus on qualities such as confidence, intelligence, and
dominance linked to leadership.
69. Fiedler’s contingency model emphasizes the match between:
A. Pay and satisfaction
B. Leader style and situational favorableness
C. Personality and integrity
D. Justice and commitment
✅Answer: B. Leader style and situational favorableness
Rationale: Fiedler proposed that leadership effectiveness depends on fit between style and
situation.
70. Transformational leaders are most likely to:
A. Rely only on punishments
B. Inspire followers through vision, intellectual stimulation, and individualized consideration
C. Avoid change
D. Focus solely on short-term exchanges
✅Answer: B. Inspire followers through vision, intellectual stimulation, and individualized
consideration
Rationale: These are hallmark features of transformational leadership.
71. Leader-member exchange theory focuses on:
A. The legal basis of leadership
B. Differences in relationships leaders form with various subordinates
C. Reinforcement schedules used by leaders
D. Whether leaders are born or made
✅Answer: B. Differences in relationships leaders form with various subordinates
Rationale: LMX theory stresses that leaders develop varying quality relationships with
different followers.
72. Power based on a leader’s expertise is called:
A. Legitimate power
B. Coercive power
C. Expert power
D. Referent power
✅Answer: C. Expert power
Rationale: Expert power comes from specialized knowledge or competence.
73. Organizational politics is best defined as:
A. Government regulation of employment
B. Informal efforts to influence others and decisions to protect or advance self-interest
C. The formal chain of command
D. Collective bargaining only
✅Answer: B. Informal efforts to influence others and decisions to protect or advance
self-interest
Rationale: Organizational politics involves influence behavior outside formal authority
structures, often self-serving.
74. Which organizational structure is characterized by many rules, high formalization, and
centralized decision-making?
A. Organic structure
B. Matrix structure
C. Mechanistic structure
D. Network structure
✅Answer: C. Mechanistic structure
Rationale: Mechanistic structures are formalized, centralized, and rule-bound.
75. Span of control refers to:
A. The number of reward systems in an organization
B. The number of subordinates reporting directly to a manager
C. The degree of task identity
D. The extent of job enlargement
✅Answer: B. The number of subordinates reporting directly to a manager
Rationale: Span of control describes the breadth of direct supervision.
76. Organizational culture refers to:
A. Nationality composition only
B. Shared assumptions, values, and norms that shape behavior
C. Written policies only
D. Formal structure and hierarchy exclusively
✅Answer: B. Shared assumptions, values, and norms that shape behavior
Rationale: Organizational culture is the shared meaning system guiding how people think and
act at work.
77. Organizational development is best described as:
A. Replacing all managers
B. Planned, organization-wide efforts to improve effectiveness and health
C. A method for computing utility tables
D. A legal approach to staffing
✅Answer: B. Planned, organization-wide efforts to improve effectiveness and health
Rationale: Organization development uses planned change to improve functioning and quality
of organizational life.
78. Which intervention is most consistent with organization development?
A. Job knowledge testing
B. Survey feedback
C. Integrity screening
D. Biodata scoring
✅Answer: B. Survey feedback
Rationale: Survey feedback is a classic OD intervention in which data are collected and fed
back for change planning.
79. A company replaces several layers of management with self-managed teams. This is most
consistent with:
A. Centralization
B. Downsizing and flatter structure
C. Scientific management only
D. Forced ranking
✅Answer: B. Downsizing and flatter structure
Rationale: Removing layers and using self-managed teams reflects a flatter organizational
form, often associated with downsizing or restructuring.
80. A test administrator allows an untrained clerk to interpret psychometric test results for job
applicants. This is most ethically problematic because it violates:
A. Impression management
B. Boundaries of competence
C. Group norms
D. Face validity
✅Answer: B. Boundaries of competence
Rationale: Allowing an unqualified person to interpret tests violates competence standards
and may harm applicants.
81. Under professional ethical principles, confidential client or examinee information may be
disclosed without consent when:
A. A friend is curious
B. A supervisor wants gossip
C. Disclosure is legally mandated or necessary to prevent serious harm
D. The psychologist wants a teaching example
✅Answer: C. Disclosure is legally mandated or necessary to prevent serious harm
Rationale: Confidentiality may be limited where law requires disclosure or where serious harm
must be prevented.
82. Which practice is most consistent with ethical test use?
A. Using any available test as long as it is inexpensive
B. Providing interpretations beyond one’s competence
C. Explaining test purposes and limitations to the examinee when appropriate
D. Posting examinee scores publicly for transparency
✅Answer: C. Explaining test purposes and limitations to the examinee when appropriate
Rationale: Ethical testing includes informed consent elements, proper explanation, and
respectful administration.
83. A psychology practitioner is asked to provide services in a new area using unfamiliar
techniques. The most ethical initial response is to:
A. Accept immediately to gain experience
B. Proceed without supervision
C. Obtain education, consultation, supervision, or make an appropriate referral
D. Delegate the work to any available employee
✅Answer: C. Obtain education, consultation, supervision, or make an appropriate referral
Rationale: Ethical practice requires working within competence and taking steps to build
competence or refer appropriately.

D. Using more than one predictor in hiring
✅Answer: B. Evaluating the job performance of one’s romantic partner in an organization

86. A registered psychometrician in the Philippines would be acting beyond proper limits if he
independently:
A. Administers and scores standardized objective tests within authorized functions

B. Conduct research only and avoid applied work
C. Apply research evidence to practice while allowing practice to inform research
D. Focus exclusively on therapy-related interventions
✅Answer: C. Apply research evidence to practice while allowing practice to inform research
Rationale: The scientist-practitioner model requires I/O psychologists to use empirical
evidence in applied settings and to let workplace problems guide further research.
6. Which research design provides the strongest basis for inferring causality?
A. Correlational design
B. Case study
C. Experimental design
D. Archival design
✅Answer: C. Experimental design
Rationale: Experimental designs manipulate an independent variable and control extraneous
influences, giving the strongest basis for causal inference.
7. A researcher finds that employees with higher job satisfaction also report lower turnover

A. Job satisfaction definitely causes lower turnover intentions
B. Turnover intentions definitely cause job satisfaction
C. The two variables are associated, but causation cannot be established from correlation
alone
D. The finding is meaningless unless an experiment is used
✅Answer: C. The two variables are associated, but causation cannot be established from
correlation alone
Rationale: A correlational result shows relationship, but direction of influence and third
variables remain uncertain.
8. Which of the following best illustrates reliability?
A. A selection test predicts job performance accurately
B. A performance rating form measures all relevant job tasks
C. An interview yields consistent scores across different trained interviewers
D. A survey looks professional and appears job related
✅Answer: C. An interview yields consistent scores across different trained interviewers
Rationale: Reliability concerns consistency. If different trained interviewers score similarly,
interrater reliability is demonstrated.
9. A measure has high validity if it:
A. Produces similar scores over repeated administrations
B. Measures what it is intended to measure
C. Contains many items
D. Is easy to administer
✅Answer: B. Measures what it is intended to measure
Rationale: Validity refers to the accuracy and appropriateness of inferences made from a
measure.
10. Meta-analysis is most useful because it:

✅Answer: B. Combines findings across studies to estimate overall trends
Rationale: Meta-analysis statistically integrates results from multiple studies to estimate the
overall strength and direction of relationships.
