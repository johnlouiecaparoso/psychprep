Question 1: Which statement best reflects the Chapter 1 definition of a psychological disorder?
(Answer: B. A psychological disorder is a psychological dysfunction associated with distress or
impairment and not merely a culturally expected response)

impairment and not merely a culturally expected response

Rationale: This definition reflects the key components of psychological disorder: dysfunction,
distress or impairment, and deviation from cultural expectations.

CHAPTER 2
CHAPTER 2: An Integrative Approach to Psychopathology

1. Which statement best reflects a multidimensional integrative approach to psychopathology?
  A. Mental disorders are caused primarily by unconscious conflict.
  B. Mental disorders result from the interaction of biological, psychological, and social

influences.
  C. Mental disorders are best explained only by neurotransmitter abnormalities.

 D. Mental disorders are largely the product of moral weakness.
       Answer: B. Mental disorders result from the interaction of biological, psychological, and
social influences.
  Rationale: The integrative approach emphasizes that psychopathology is best understood
through interacting biological, psychological, and social factors rather than a single-cause
model.

2. A clinician explains that a person's panic symptoms emerged only after a major stressor
activated a preexisting vulnerability. Which model is best illustrated?

  A. Classical conditioning model
  B. Diathesis-stress model
  C. Humanistic model

 D. Moral model
       Answer: B. Diathesis-stress model
  Rationale: The diathesis-stress model proposes that an inherited or acquired vulnerability may
remain latent until activated by a stressor, leading to disorder.

3. Which statement best describes polygenic influence?
  A. A single gene completely determines one psychological disorder.
  B. Several unrelated environments replace genetic influence.
  C. Many genes contribute small effects to behavioral and psychological traits.

 D. Genes affect only physical, not psychological, characteristics.
       Answer: C. Many genes contribute small effects to behavioral and psychological traits.
  Rationale: Polygenic influence means complex traits, including many forms of
psychopathology, are shaped by multiple genes with small individual effects.

4. A child with a genetic vulnerability for conduct problems also tends to evoke harsh reactions
from others, increasing exposure to stress that strengthens the problem. This is best described
as:

  A. Extinction
  B. Reciprocal gene-environment correlation
  C. Negative reinforcement

 D. Circular reasoning
       Answer: B. Reciprocal gene-environment correlation
  Rationale: This model describes how genetic tendencies may increase exposure to
environmental experiences that then reinforce the disorder process.
5. Which statement best describes epigenetics?
  A. Permanent alteration of DNA sequence by psychotherapy
  B. Environmental effects on whether certain genes are expressed
  C. Inheritance of disorders through one dominant gene only

 D. Elimination of biological influences by social learning
       Answer: B. Environmental effects on whether certain genes are expressed
  Rationale: Epigenetics concerns how environmental influences such as stress may switch
genes on or off without changing the DNA sequence itself.

6. Which conclusion is most consistent with contemporary views of genetic contributions to
psychopathology?

  A. Genetic vulnerability guarantees later disorder.
  B. Environment is irrelevant when heritability is high.
  C. Genetic and environmental influences interact in complex ways.

 D. Psychological disorders are inherited in exactly the same way as eye color.
       Answer: C. Genetic and environmental influences interact in complex ways.
  Rationale: Contemporary psychopathology rejects strict nature-versus-nurture thinking and
instead emphasizes interaction between vulnerability and experience.

7. Which nervous system division is primarily responsible for coordinating basic bodily functions
and transmitting information within the brain and spinal cord?

  A. Peripheral nervous system
  B. Somatic nervous system
  C. Central nervous system

 D. Autonomic nervous system
       Answer: C. Central nervous system
  Rationale: The central nervous system consists of the brain and spinal cord and coordinates
fundamental processing relevant to behavior and psychopathology.

8. Which neurotransmitter has most often been linked to mood regulation and has been
prominently implicated in depression and related emotional disorders?

  A. Serotonin
  B. Acetylcholine
  C. Endorphin

 D. Histamine
       Answer: A. Serotonin
  Rationale: Serotonin is strongly associated with mood regulation and has been widely studied
in depression and emotional disorders.

9. Gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) is best described as a neurotransmitter that is primarily:
  A. Excitatory and increases overall neural firing
  B. Inhibitory and helps regulate excessive neural activity
  C. Responsible only for motor movement
  D. Unrelated to anxiety and mood processes
  Answer: B. Inhibitory and helps regulate excessive neural activity

  Rationale: GABA is the major inhibitory neurotransmitter and is especially important in
regulating anxiety and arousal.

10. Dopamine dysfunction has been most strongly associated with which clinical phenomenon?
   A. Specific phobia
   B. Psychotic symptoms such as delusions and hallucinations
   C. Dissociative fugue only

 D. Somatic symptom disorder only
       Answer: B. Psychotic symptoms such as delusions and hallucinations
   Rationale: Dopamine dysregulation has long been associated with psychotic symptoms,
especially in schizophrenia spectrum disorders.

11. Which statement best captures the role of brain circuits in psychopathology?
   A. One isolated brain area fully explains each mental disorder.
   B. Psychological disorders often involve dysregulation in interconnected neural pathways.
   C. Brain circuits matter only in neurocognitive disorders.

 D. Brain circuits are clinically irrelevant because behavior is learned.
       Answer: B. Psychological disorders often involve dysregulation in interconnected neural
pathways.
   Rationale: Modern neuroscience emphasizes circuits and networks rather than single isolated
regions as the basis for many disorders.

   A. A purely biological model

13. Which learning principle is best illustrated when a person develops intense fear after pairing

   B. Classical conditioning
   C. Modeling

14. A student starts avoiding class presentations after observing a classmate being ridiculed

1. A client persistently complains of multiple physical symptoms that disrupt daily functioning.

  Rationale: Somatic symptom disorder involves one or more distressing somatic symptoms

not simply the absence of a medical explanation but the maladaptive psychological response to
the symptoms.

2. Which feature best distinguishes illness anxiety disorder from somatic symptom disorder?

somatic symptoms
  Rationale: Illness anxiety disorder is distinguished by preoccupation with illness and disease
conviction, often despite little or no substantial somatic symptom burden. Somatic symptom
disorder, by contrast, includes distressing somatic symptoms themselves.

3. A patient becomes convinced that mild intermittent abdominal discomfort signals colon

  A. Somatic symptom disorder
  B. Conversion disorder
  C. Illness anxiety disorder

 D. Factitious disorder
       Answer: C. Illness anxiety disorder
  Rationale: The primary feature is persistent fear of serious disease despite minimal findings
and repeated reassurance. The client's focus is on the feared illness rather than on severe
somatic symptoms themselves.

4. Which statement best reflects the contemporary diagnostic view of somatic symptom
disorder?

  A. It is diagnosed only when no medical explanation can be found
  B. It is diagnosed primarily on the basis of excessive psychological response to somatic
symptoms

 D. It is limited to pain complaints
       Answer: B. It is diagnosed primarily on the basis of excessive psychological response to
somatic symptoms
  Rationale: Current diagnostic formulation emphasizes positive clinical features: distressing
somatic symptoms plus disproportionate thoughts, anxiety, or behaviors related to them. This
avoids the old overreliance on symptoms being medically unexplained.

  A. Illness anxiety disorder
  B. Factitious disorder
  C. Conversion disorder

 D. Somatic symptom disorder
       Answer: C. Conversion disorder
  Rationale: Conversion disorder involves genuinely experienced neurological-like symptoms,
such as blindness or paralysis, that are not intentionally produced and are not supported by
recognized neurological pathology.

6. Which clinical presentation is most characteristic of conversion disorder?
  A. Excessive fear of having a disease despite little or no somatic symptoms
  B. Physical malfunctioning without corresponding physical pathology
  C. Intentional symptom fabrication for financial gain

 D. Chronic worry about appearance flaws
       Answer: B. Physical malfunctioning without corresponding physical pathology
  Rationale: Conversion disorder is classically characterized by physical dysfunction, often
sensory or motor, that lacks corresponding recognized pathology and is not intentionally
produced.

7. Which factor most directly distinguishes malingering from factitious disorder?
  A. Presence of dissociation
  B. Severity of physical complaints
  C. External incentive for symptom production

 D. History of trauma
       Answer: C. External incentive for symptom production
  Rationale: Malingering is distinguished from factitious disorder by the presence of clear
external reward, such as money, avoidance of duty, or legal advantage. Factitious disorder lacks
such obvious external incentives.

  B. Factitious disorder
  C. Dissociative amnesia

 D. Malingering
       Answer: D. Malingering
  Rationale: The man is feigning symptoms for compensation, which is a clear external
incentive. That pattern is most consistent with malingering.

  A. Factitious disorder
  B. Illness anxiety disorder
  C. Somatic symptom disorder

 D. Malingering
       Answer: A. Factitious disorder
  Rationale: Factitious disorder involves intentional falsification or induction of symptoms in the
absence of obvious external incentives. The goal is often to assume the sick role.

1. A 19-year-old college student has become progressively underweight after months of severe
food restriction. She insists she is still "too big," becomes distressed when asked to gain weight,
and repeatedly weighs herself despite being medically underweight. Which diagnosis is most
consistent with this presentation?

A. Bulimia nervosa
B. Anorexia nervosa
C. Binge-eating disorder
D. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder

 Answer: B. Anorexia nervosa

Rationale: Anorexia nervosa is characterized by significantly low body weight, intense fear of
gaining weight or persistent behavior interfering with weight gain, and disturbance in the way
body weight or shape is experienced.

2. A client reports recurrent episodes of consuming unusually large amounts of food within a
short period while feeling unable to stop. She then induces vomiting and misuses laxatives to
prevent weight gain. These episodes occur at least once weekly for 4 months. Which diagnosis
is most appropriate?

A. Bulimia nervosa
B. Binge-eating disorder
C. Anorexia nervosa, restricting type
D. Rumination disorder

 Answer: A. Bulimia nervosa

Rationale: Bulimia nervosa requires recurrent binge eating, recurrent inappropriate
compensatory behaviors to prevent weight gain, and occurrence at least once weekly for 3
months.

3. A 28-year-old woman has recurrent binge-eating episodes associated with guilt, eating
rapidly, and eating alone due to embarrassment. She does not purge, fast, or exercise
excessively to compensate. Which diagnosis is most likely?

A. Bulimia nervosa
B. Binge-eating disorder
C. Anorexia nervosa
D. Pica

 Answer: B. Binge-eating disorder
Rationale: Binge-eating disorder involves recurrent binge episodes with loss of control,
associated features such as guilt, rapid eating, and eating alone, plus marked distress, without
recurrent compensatory behaviors.

4. A 10-year-old child persistently refuses many foods because of their texture, smell, and color.
He has lost weight, developed nutritional deficiency, and requires supplements, but there is no
fear of fatness or body-image disturbance. Which diagnosis is most appropriate?

A. Anorexia nervosa
B. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder
C. Bulimia nervosa
D. Oppositional defiant disorder

 Answer: B. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder

Rationale: Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder involves restricted intake due to sensory
features, aversive consequences, or low interest in food, resulting in weight loss, nutritional
deficiency, supplements, or psychosocial impairment, without body-image disturbance.

5. Which feature best distinguishes avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder from anorexia
nervosa?

A. Weight loss is absent in avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder
B. Nutritional deficiency occurs only in anorexia nervosa
C. Body-image disturbance and fear of weight gain are absent in avoidant/restrictive food intake
disorder
D. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder occurs only in childhood

 Answer: C. Body-image disturbance and fear of weight gain are absent in avoidant/restrictive

food intake disorder
Rationale: The crucial distinction is that avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder does not involve
disturbance in body image or fear of becoming fat, whereas anorexia nervosa does.

6. A patient repeatedly regurgitates recently ingested food, rechews it, and spits it out. The
behavior is not due to a gastrointestinal disorder and is not primarily motivated by fear of weight
gain. Which diagnosis is most consistent?

A. Bulimia nervosa
B. Rumination disorder
C. Pica
D. Binge-eating disorder

 Answer: B. Rumination disorder
Rationale: Rumination disorder involves repeated regurgitation of food that may be rechewed,
reswallowed, or spit out and is not better explained by a gastrointestinal disorder or solely by
another feeding/eating disorder.

7. Which statement about binge-eating disorder is most accurate?

A. It requires recurrent compensatory behavior
B. It is diagnosed only when obesity is present
C. Marked distress about binge eating is required
D. Body weight must be significantly below normal

 Answer: C. Marked distress about binge eating is required

Rationale: Marked distress regarding binge eating is a required criterion for binge-eating
disorder and helps distinguish it from occasional overeating.

8. Which finding most strongly supports anorexia nervosa rather than bulimia nervosa?

A. Recurrent binge eating
B. Overconcern with body shape and weight
C. Self-induced vomiting
D. Significantly low body weight

 Answer: D. Significantly low body weight

Rationale: Both anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa may involve body-shape overvaluation
and even binge/purge behaviors, but anorexia nervosa requires significantly low body weight.

9. A woman lost considerable weight through severe dietary restriction but remains within the
normal BMI range. She has intense fear of gaining weight and body-image disturbance. Which
diagnosis is most appropriate?

A. Anorexia nervosa
B. Other specified feeding or eating disorder, atypical anorexia nervosa
C. Bulimia nervosa
D. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder

 Answer: B. Other specified feeding or eating disorder, atypical anorexia nervosa

Rationale: Atypical anorexia nervosa is used when all features of anorexia nervosa are present
except that, despite significant weight loss, body weight remains within or above the normal
range.

10. Which complication is classically associated with recurrent self-induced vomiting in bulimia
nervosa?

A. Hyperglycemia
B. Loss of dental enamel
C. Increased bone density
D. Improved cardiac conduction

 Answer: B. Loss of dental enamel

Rationale: Repeated vomiting in bulimia nervosa may erode dental enamel, especially on the
lingual surfaces of the front teeth.

11. Which of the following is most characteristic of bulimia nervosa rather than binge-eating
disorder?

A. Binge eating with loss of control
B. Feelings of shame after overeating
C. Recurrent inappropriate compensatory behaviors
D. Marked distress about binge eating

 Answer: C. Recurrent inappropriate compensatory behaviors

Rationale: The defining difference is that bulimia nervosa includes recurrent behaviors such as
vomiting, laxative misuse, fasting, or excessive exercise to prevent weight gain.

1. Which statement best reflects the scope of health psychology?
  A. It studies only psychiatric effects of medical illness
  B. It focuses on psychological factors involved in health promotion and maintenance
  C. It is limited to neuropsychological testing in hospital settings

 D. It is primarily concerned with classifying mental disorders
       Answer: B. It focuses on psychological factors involved in health promotion and
maintenance
  Rationale: Health psychology is concerned with psychological factors important in promoting
health and maintaining well-being. It is broader than illness treatment alone and includes
prevention and behavior change.

2. Behavioral medicine is best described as the:
  A. study of unconscious conflicts in chronic disease
  B. branch of medicine limited to pharmacologic treatment adherence
  C. application of behavioral science techniques to prevent, diagnose, and treat medical

problems

 D. use of projective tests in rehabilitation settings
       Answer: C. application of behavioral science techniques to prevent, diagnose, and treat
medical problems
  Rationale: Behavioral medicine applies behavioral science to medical problems, including
prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of physical disorders.

3. Which pathway best explains how psychosocial factors may directly affect physical health?
  A. They always produce structural organ damage without biological mediation
  B. They alter biological processes such as immune and nervous system functioning
  C. They influence health only by changing physician behavior

 D. They affect health only through malingering
       Answer: B. They alter biological processes such as immune and nervous system
functioning
  Rationale: Psychosocial factors can directly influence health by affecting basic biological
systems, especially the nervous and immune systems, not merely by influencing habits.

4. A major feature of the stress response is activation of the:
  A. parasympathetic nervous system alone
  B. occipital-limbic reflex arc
  C. hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenocortical axis

 D. extrapyramidal motor system
       Answer: C. hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenocortical axis
  Rationale: A key component of the stress response is activation of the HPA axis, which leads
to hormonal changes associated with arousal and adaptation to threat.
5. Which condition is most consistent with chronic stress exposure?
  A. Sustained cortisol secretion with possible harmful long-term effects
  B. Permanent elimination of autonomic arousal
  C. Immediate strengthening of all immune responses

 D. Loss of all emotional reactivity
       Answer: A. Sustained cortisol secretion with possible harmful long-term effects
  Rationale: Chronic stress is associated with prolonged cortisol activity, which may contribute
to harmful physiological consequences over time.

6. Which statement best reflects the relationship among stress, emotion, and illness?
  A. Excitement is always protective and unrelated to illness
  B. Anxiety, depression, stress, and low perceived control may weaken health-related biological

functioning
  C. Only anger has clinically significant health effects

 D. Emotional states affect mental but not physical disorders
       Answer: B. Anxiety, depression, stress, and low perceived control may weaken
health-related biological functioning
  Rationale: Negative emotional states and reduced perceived control are associated with
poorer biological functioning and increased vulnerability to illness.

7. The field that studies interactions among psychological processes, the nervous system, and
the immune system is:

  A. psychopharmacology
  B. psychometrics
  C. psychoneuroimmunology

 D. neuropsychoanalysis
       Answer: C. psychoneuroimmunology
  Rationale: Psychoneuroimmunology examines how psychological variables interact with
nervous-system and immune-system processes.

8. An autoimmune disease is present when:
  A. the nervous system attacks the endocrine glands
  B. the body's immune system attacks its own healthy tissue
  C. antibodies can no longer be produced

 D. viral replication has ceased completely
       Answer: B. the body's immune system attacks its own healthy tissue
  Rationale: An autoimmune disease occurs when immune defenses mistakenly attack normal
body tissue rather than foreign antigens.

9. Which statement about risky behavior and disease is most accurate?
  A. Risky behavior is unrelated to major causes of illness
  B. Many major causes of death are linked to lifestyle factors such as smoking, diet, and

physical inactivity
  C. Lifestyle influences only infectious disease
 D. Health outcomes are determined only by heredity
       Answer: B. Many major causes of death are linked to lifestyle factors such as smoking,
diet, and physical inactivity
  Rationale: Major contributors to illness and mortality are strongly associated with modifiable
lifestyle factors, making prevention and behavior change central.

10. Which situation best illustrates a psychosocial contribution to disease progression?
   A. A patient's blood type predicts treatment preference
   B. Persistent stress contributes to recurrence of herpes outbreaks
   C. A fracture heals according to bone density alone

 D. A cataract causes auditory hallucinations
       Answer: B. Persistent stress contributes to recurrence of herpes outbreaks
   Rationale: This example demonstrates how stress can worsen a physical condition by
affecting biological vulnerability and disease course.

11. Which statement best describes acquired immune deficiency syndrome?
   A. It is caused by elevated blood pressure alone
   B. It primarily involves destruction of the immune system, allowing opportunistic infections to

develop
   C. It is mainly a sleep-wake disorder

 D. It is defined by delusions of contamination
       Answer: B. It primarily involves destruction of the immune system, allowing opportunistic
infections to develop
   Rationale: AIDS compromises immune functioning, which leaves the body vulnerable to
opportunistic infections that can progress uncontrollably.

12. In AIDS prevention, the most effective psychological approach primarily emphasizes:
   A. dream interpretation
   B. behavior modification targeting high-risk practices
   C. aversive conditioning for all sexual behavior

 D. unrestricted disclosure of serostatus to employers
       Answer: B. behavior modification targeting high-risk practices
   Rationale: Because prevention is crucial in HIV/AIDS, psychological intervention emphasizes
changing unsafe sexual and drug-use behaviors.

13. Which finding best supports the role of psychological factors in AIDS outcomes?
   A. Immune robustness and sense of control may influence disease course
   B. AIDS progression is entirely unaffected by stress
   C. Psychological factors matter only after full recovery

 D. Counseling replaces antiretroviral treatment
       Answer: A. Immune robustness and sense of control may influence disease course
   Rationale: Psychological factors such as perceived control, along with stronger immune
functioning, may affect progression and adjustment in AIDS.
14. Psychoncology is concerned with:
   A. legal competence in oncology wards
   B. psychosocial factors involved in the course and treatment of cancer
   C. tumor classification based on histology alone

 D. malingering among cancer patients
       Answer: B. psychosocial factors involved in the course and treatment of cancer
   Rationale: Psychoncology studies how psychological and social variables affect cancer
adjustment, treatment, symptom burden, and sometimes outcome.

15. Which statement is most accurate regarding psychosocial treatment in cancer care?
   A. It replaces medical treatment for malignant tumors
   B. It may prolong life, alleviate symptoms, and reduce depression and pain
   C. It is useful only for children

 D. It is contraindicated when distress is present
       Answer: B. It may prolong life, alleviate symptoms, and reduce depression and pain
   Rationale: Psychosocial interventions in cancer care can improve quality of life, lessen
distress and pain, and may contribute to better outcomes in some cases.

16. Which physical disorder is most closely associated with persistent constriction of blood
vessels that increases cardiac workload?

   A. Hypertension
   B. Narcolepsy
   C. Multiple sclerosis

 D. Somatic symptom disorder
       Answer: A. Hypertension
   Rationale: Hypertension involves persistently elevated blood pressure, often associated with
vascular constriction and increased cardiac workload.

17. Coronary heart disease is best described as:
   A. a disorder of excessive sleepiness caused by circadian disruption
   B. blockage of arteries supplying blood to the heart
   C. chronic muscular pain without tissue pathology

 D. fear of having a heart attack despite normal health
       Answer: B. blockage of arteries supplying blood to the heart
   Rationale: Coronary heart disease develops when blood flow to the heart muscle is
compromised by arterial blockage.

18. Which behavioral style was historically linked to elevated risk for coronary heart disease?
   A. Type B behavior pattern
   B. Avoidant behavior pattern
   C. Type A behavior pattern

 D. Cyclothymic behavior pattern
       Answer: C. Type A behavior pattern
   Rationale: Type A behavior, especially chronic hostility, urgency, and competitiveness, has
historically been associated with greater risk for coronary heart disease.

19. Which emotional factors have also been implicated in coronary heart disease?
   A. Anxiety and depression
   B. Euphoria and curiosity
   C. Dissociation and amnesia

 D. Obsessions and compulsions only
       Answer: A. Anxiety and depression
   Rationale: Research has implicated anxiety and depression, in addition to anger and hostility,
in cardiovascular risk and poorer cardiac outcomes.

20. A patient becomes angry, impatient, competitive, and chronically time-urgent. This pattern is
most consistent with:

   A. dependent behavior pattern
   B. type A behavior pattern
   C. type B behavior pattern

 D. schizoid behavior pattern
       Answer: B. type A behavior pattern
   Rationale: Time urgency, competitiveness, impatience, and anger-proneness are
characteristic of the Type A pattern.

21. Chronic pain is best defined as pain that:
   A. always has no physical basis
   B. disappears quickly once tissue injury heals
   C. persists over time and may be influenced by social and psychological factors

 D. occurs only in terminal illness
       Answer: C. persists over time and may be influenced by social and psychological factors
   Rationale: Chronic pain is enduring pain that may remain over time and is shaped not only by
physical variables but also by stress, coping, and interpersonal factors.

22. Which statement best distinguishes chronic pain from acute pain?
   A. Chronic pain is always psychotic in origin
   B. Chronic pain may continue even when acute tissue healing should have occurred
   C. Acute pain is always less intense

 D. Chronic pain cannot be treated psychologically
       Answer: B. Chronic pain may continue even when acute tissue healing should have
occurred
   Rationale: A key distinction is that chronic pain persists beyond the expected healing period
and becomes maintained by multiple processes.

23. Which treatment principle is most appropriate for chronic pain?
   A. Focus exclusively on tissue pathology
   B. Address pain-related stress, coping, and perceived control in addition to medical factors
   C. Avoid all behavioral interventions

 D. Withhold information to reduce symptom focus
       Answer: B. Address pain-related stress, coping, and perceived control in addition to
medical factors
   Rationale: Effective chronic pain management is biopsychosocial and includes stress
reduction, coping skills, and restoration of control alongside medical care.

24. Chronic fatigue syndrome is characterized by:
   A. transient sadness after exertion
   B. incapacitating exhaustion after minimal exertion, often with pain and emotional symptoms
   C. a primary psychotic process

 D. repeated sleep paralysis only
       Answer: B. incapacitating exhaustion after minimal exertion, often with pain and emotional
symptoms
   Rationale: Chronic fatigue syndrome involves severe exhaustion disproportionate to exertion
and may include pain, headaches, depression, and anxiety.

25. Which intervention most directly teaches a patient to observe and modify physiological
responses such as muscle tension or heart rate?

   A. Flooding
   B. Biofeedback
   C. Free association

 D. Response cost
       Answer: B. Biofeedback
   Rationale: Biofeedback uses monitoring equipment to make physiological processes visible
so patients can learn to regulate them.

26. Which statement best describes relaxation training and meditation in health psychology?
   A. They are used only for insomnia
   B. They may reduce stress-related arousal and increase perceived control
   C. They are forms of neurological surgery

 D. They are inappropriate for pain management
       Answer: B. They may reduce stress-related arousal and increase perceived control
   Rationale: Relaxation and meditation help reduce arousal and foster calm, coping, and a
stronger sense of control over stress reactions.

27. Comprehensive stress- and pain-reduction programs are generally:
   A. less effective than single isolated techniques
   B. more effective because they combine multiple coping and behavioral strategies
   C. limited to medication management

 D. inappropriate for medically ill clients
       Answer: B. more effective because they combine multiple coping and behavioral
strategies
   Rationale: Comprehensive programs tend to outperform single techniques because they
address stress, cognition, coping, and behavior together.

28. Which prevention strategy best reflects a health psychology approach?
   A. Waiting until disease becomes severe before intervening
   B. Changing unsafe behaviors related to sexual practices, substance use, diet, exercise, and

injury risk
   C. Avoiding public health education because it biases behavior

 D. Using diagnosis alone to reduce mortality
       Answer: B. Changing unsafe behaviors related to sexual practices, substance use, diet,
exercise, and injury risk
   Rationale: Health psychology emphasizes prevention by targeting modifiable behaviors that
increase disease and injury risk.

29. A psychometrician in the Philippines is asked to independently provide psychotherapy for a
patient with cancer-related distress. What is the most appropriate response?

   A. Provide psychotherapy independently because distress is not a mental disorder
   B. Provide psychotherapy only if the client verbally agrees
   C. Decline independent therapy practice and refer or work within legally permitted supervised
boundaries

 D. Proceed if no psychologist is available nearby, regardless of law
       Answer: C. Decline independent therapy practice and refer or work within legally permitted
supervised boundaries
   Rationale: Under Philippine ethical and legal standards, psychometricians practice within
prescribed boundaries and are prohibited from independently practicing therapy except in
emergencies. Proper referral or supervised lawful practice is required.

30. Which ethical principle is most directly involved when handling a client's health-related
assessment data?

   A. Confidentiality
   B. Beneficence only
   C. Test popularity

 D. Examiner convenience
       Answer: A. Confidentiality
   Rationale: Health-related assessment data are protected by the duty of confidentiality, with
disclosure restricted and limited to lawful or ethically justified circumstances.

31. A psychometrician is asked to administer an assessment tool that is outdated and lacks
appropriate norms for the client. The most ethical action is to:

   A. administer it if the referral source insists
   B. use it and simply avoid interpretation
   C. avoid using the obsolete or inappropriate test

 D. photocopy a more familiar version and proceed
       Answer: C. avoid using the obsolete or inappropriate test
   Rationale: Ethical assessment requires the use of tests that are current, valid, reliable, and
appropriate for the client. Obsolete or normatively inappropriate tests should not be used as the
basis for conclusions.

32. Which statement best reflects ethical informed consent in assessment?
   A. Consent is unnecessary if the examiner is licensed
   B. Clients should be educated about the nature of services, risks, financial arrangements, and

limits of confidentiality
   C. Only written consent counts as valid

 D. Results may be released freely after testing
       Answer: B. Clients should be educated about the nature of services, risks, financial
arrangements, and limits of confidentiality
   Rationale: Informed consent requires that clients understand the service, its risks,
arrangements, and confidentiality limits before assessment proceeds.

33. A psychometrician is working with a medically ill client referred by a hospital. Before
providing services, the practitioner should clarify:

   A. only the physician's diagnosis
   B. the expected role, who the client is, probable uses of information, and limits of

   C. only the client's payment source

 D. only whether therapy will be needed later
       Answer: B. the expected role, who the client is, probable uses of information, and limits of
confidentiality
   Rationale: In third-party referrals, ethical practice requires clarifying roles, client identity,
expected uses of information, and confidentiality limits from the outset.

34. Which practice is most consistent with Philippine ethical standards on assessment?
   A. Releasing raw test data to any relative who asks
   B. Using only original test materials and securing test records from unqualified persons
   C. Allowing untrained staff to interpret protocols for efficiency

 D. Explaining results only in highly technical language
       Answer: B. Using only original test materials and securing test records from unqualified
persons
   Rationale: Ethical assessment practice requires proper test security, use of original materials,
and protection of protocols and records from unauthorized access.

35. A psychometrician involved in a hospital-based wellness program for clients with
hypertension is asked to deliver a service beyond current competence in behavioral health
intervention. What is the best course of action?

   A. Continue because any licensed practitioner may perform all services
   B. Provide the service without limits to avoid losing the client
   C. Obtain appropriate training or supervision and make a proper referral if necessary
   D. Delegate the work to any available staff member
  Answer: C. Obtain appropriate training or supervision and make a proper referral if

   Rationale: When a service exceeds current competence, the practitioner must seek training,

supervision, consultation, or referral. This protects client welfare and remains consistent with
professional boundaries.

CHAPTER 10: Sexual Dysfunctions, Paraphilic Disorders, and Gender Dysphoria

1. Which statement best distinguishes a sexual dysfunction from ordinary variation in sexual
experience?

  A. Any reduction in sexual frequency automatically indicates a disorder.
  B. A diagnosis requires a clinically significant disturbance in sexual response or pleasure, not
merely limited experience or inadequate stimulation.
  C. Any sexual concern reported by one partner is sufficient for diagnosis.

 D. A diagnosis is made whenever sexual behavior differs from cultural expectations.
       Answer: B. A diagnosis requires a clinically significant disturbance in sexual response or
pleasure, not merely limited experience or inadequate stimulation.
  Rationale: Sexual dysfunctions involve a clinically significant disturbance in the ability to
respond sexually or experience pleasure. Clinical judgment is also needed to determine whether
the problem is better explained by inadequate stimulation or lack of knowledge rather than a
disorder.

2. A clinician evaluating a client's sexual complaint notes that the problem has been present
since the client's earliest sexual experiences. Which subtype is most appropriate?

  A. Situational
  B. Acquired
  C. Lifelong

 D. Generalized
       Answer: C. Lifelong
  Rationale: "Lifelong" is used when the sexual problem has been present from the individual's
first sexual experiences.

3. A man reports erectile difficulty only with one partner but not during masturbation or with
previous partners. Which subtype best describes this presentation?

  A. Lifelong
  B. Generalized
  C. Situational

 D. Persistent
       Answer: C. Situational
  Rationale: Situational sexual dysfunction occurs only under certain circumstances, such as
with one partner but not others.

4. Which pair of specifiers is commonly used in describing sexual dysfunctions?
  A. Acute/chronic and mild/severe
  B. Lifelong/acquired and generalized/situational
  C. Psychotic/nonpsychotic and recurrent/single episode

 D. Internalizing/externalizing and trait/state
       Answer: B. Lifelong/acquired and generalized/situational
  Rationale: DSM-based description of sexual dysfunctions commonly uses onset specifiers
(lifelong/acquired) and contextual specifiers (generalized/situational).

5. Which of the following is the best first step in the assessment of a sexual complaint?
  A. Immediate referral for surgery
  B. A detailed interview exploring the nature, onset, context, and course of the problem
  C. Automatic administration of a projective test

 D. Immediate diagnosis based only on the chief complaint
       Answer: B. A detailed interview exploring the nature, onset, context, and course of the
problem
  Rationale: Interview is a foundational assessment method for sexual complaints because it
clarifies onset, severity, context, relationship issues, medical factors, and associated distress.

6. Which assessment method is most directly used to evaluate physiological aspects of sexual
arousal?

  A. Mental status examination
  B. Sociogram
  C. Psychophysiological assessment

 D. Intelligence testing
       Answer: C. Psychophysiological assessment
  Rationale: Psychophysiological assessment directly examines physiological components of
sexual arousal and response.

7. Which factor should be considered in the clinical assessment of sexual dysfunction because it
may contribute to etiology or treatment?

  A. Partner factors only
  B. Biological factors only
  C. Relationship, individual vulnerability, cultural, and medical factors

 D. Astrological compatibility
       Answer: C. Relationship, individual vulnerability, cultural, and medical factors
  Rationale: Assessment should consider partner factors, relationship dynamics, individual
vulnerabilities, psychiatric comorbidity, stressors, cultural or religious influences, and relevant
medical conditions.

8. A client reports inability to reach orgasm because neither the client nor the partner has
knowledge of effective sexual stimulation. Which is the best diagnostic conclusion?

  A. Sexual dysfunction should automatically be diagnosed
  B. A paraphilic disorder should be diagnosed
  C. Clinical judgment may indicate need for care, but a sexual dysfunction diagnosis may not
be warranted

 D. Gender dysphoria should be ruled in first
       Answer: C. Clinical judgment may indicate need for care, but a sexual dysfunction
diagnosis may not be warranted
  Rationale: DSM-based guidance notes that lack of effective stimulation or knowledge may
warrant care or psychoeducation, but does not necessarily justify a sexual dysfunction
diagnosis.

9. Which statement best reflects the multidimensional understanding of sexual function?
  A. Sexual function is purely biological
  B. Sexual function is purely learned
  C. Sexual function reflects the interaction of biological, psychological, sociocultural, and

interpersonal factors

 D. Sexual function is determined only by religious upbringing
       Answer: C. Sexual function reflects the interaction of biological, psychological,
sociocultural, and interpersonal factors
  Rationale: Sexual functioning is multidetermined and cannot be adequately understood
through a one-factor explanation.

10. Which condition is classified as a sexual dysfunction in DSM-5-TR?
   A. Gender dysphoria
   B. Fetishistic disorder
   C. Female sexual interest/arousal disorder

 D. Voyeuristic disorder
       Answer: C. Female sexual interest/arousal disorder
   Rationale: Female sexual interest/arousal disorder is one of the sexual dysfunctions listed in
DSM-5-TR.

11. Which condition is classified as a paraphilic disorder rather than a sexual dysfunction?
   A. Premature (early) ejaculation
   B. Female orgasmic disorder
   C. Fetishistic disorder

 D. Genito-pelvic pain/penetration disorder
       Answer: C. Fetishistic disorder
   Rationale: Fetishistic disorder belongs to the paraphilic disorders, not the sexual
dysfunctions.

12. Which condition is characterized by recurrent and intense sexual arousal from touching or
rubbing against a nonconsenting person?

   A. Voyeuristic disorder
   B. Fetishistic disorder
   C. Frotteuristic disorder

 D. Transvestic disorder
       Answer: C. Frotteuristic disorder
   Rationale: Frotteuristic disorder involves recurrent and intense sexual arousal from touching
or rubbing against a nonconsenting person.
13. Which disorder is most associated with exposing one's genitals to an unsuspecting
nonconsenting person?

   A. Exhibitionistic disorder
   B. Voyeuristic disorder
   C. Sexual sadism disorder

 D. Pedophilic disorder
       Answer: A. Exhibitionistic disorder
   Rationale: Exhibitionistic disorder is characterized by sexual arousal associated with
exposing the genitals to a nonconsenting person.

14. Which disorder is most associated with recurrent and intense sexual arousal from observing
an unsuspecting person who is naked, disrobing, or engaged in sexual activity?

   A. Frotteuristic disorder
   B. Voyeuristic disorder
   C. Fetishistic disorder

 D. Transvestic disorder
       Answer: B. Voyeuristic disorder
   Rationale: Voyeuristic disorder centers on sexual arousal from observing an unsuspecting
person who is naked, disrobing, or sexually active.

15. Which statement best distinguishes paraphilia from paraphilic disorder?
   A. A paraphilia always requires hospitalization
   B. A paraphilic disorder exists only when the sexual interest is illegal
   C. A paraphilia becomes a disorder when it causes distress or impairment to the individual or

its satisfaction entails harm or risk of harm to others

 D. There is no distinction between the two terms
       Answer: C. A paraphilia becomes a disorder when it causes distress or impairment to the
individual or its satisfaction entails harm or risk of harm to others
   Rationale: DSM distinguishes paraphilia from paraphilic disorder. The interest alone is not
enough; disorder requires distress, impairment, or harm/risk of harm to others.

16. Which grouping correctly classifies voyeuristic, exhibitionistic, and frotteuristic disorders?
   A. Gender identity disorders
   B. Courtship disorders
   C. Orgasm disorders

 D. Dissociative disorders
       Answer: B. Courtship disorders
   Rationale: Voyeuristic, exhibitionistic, and frotteuristic disorders are grouped as courtship
disorders because they resemble distorted components of human courtship behavior.

17. Which grouping correctly classifies sexual sadism disorder and sexual masochism disorder?
   A. Courtship disorders
   B. Target-preference disorders
   C. Algolagnic disorders
 D. Dissociative disorders
       Answer: C. Algolagnic disorders
   Rationale: Sexual sadism disorder and sexual masochism disorder are algolagnic disorders
because they involve pain, suffering, humiliation, or bondage.

18. Which statement is most accurate regarding fetishistic disorder?
   A. Any preference for a partner's body part is automatically a disorder
   B. It may involve nonliving objects or a highly specific focus on a nongenital body part, but

diagnosis requires clinically significant distress or impairment
   C. It is diagnosed only when cross-dressing is present

 D. It is more common in women than in men in clinical samples
       Answer: B. It may involve nonliving objects or a highly specific focus on a nongenital body
part, but diagnosis requires clinically significant distress or impairment
   Rationale: Fetishistic disorder may involve nonliving objects or a nongenital body part as the
primary focus of arousal, but diagnosis requires clinically significant distress or impairment.

19. Which of the following is the nearest diagnostic neighbor of fetishistic disorder when fetish
objects are limited to clothing worn during cross-dressing?

   A. Voyeuristic disorder
   B. Pedophilic disorder
   C. Transvestic disorder

 D. Sexual sadism disorder
       Answer: C. Transvestic disorder
   Rationale: When fetish objects are limited to clothing worn during cross-dressing, the
differential diagnosis most closely points to transvestic disorder.

20. Which statement best reflects the DSM-5-TR view of pedophilic disorder?
   A. Any sexual offense against a minor automatically proves pedophilic disorder
   B. The diagnosis may be considered when there is recurrent, intense sexual arousal involving

prepubescent children, together with distress, impairment, or acted-on urges
   C. Childhood sexual abuse is an established sole cause

 D. The diagnosis is limited to women
       Answer: B. The diagnosis may be considered when there is recurrent, intense sexual
arousal involving prepubescent children, together with distress, impairment, or acted-on urges
   Rationale: Pedophilic disorder requires a persistent pedophilic interest plus clinically
significant distress/impairment or acted-on urges, not merely a single allegation or offense.

21. A man sexually abuses a child during intoxication, but there is no evidence of recurrent
preferential attraction to prepubescent children over time. Which conclusion is best?

   A. Pedophilic disorder is automatically diagnosed
   B. Only antisocial personality disorder can be diagnosed
   C. Pedophilic disorder should not be diagnosed without evidence of persistent pedophilic
interest meeting full criteria
   D. Gender dysphoria should be considered first
  Answer: C. Pedophilic disorder should not be diagnosed without evidence of persistent

pedophilic interest meeting full criteria
   Rationale: A sexual offense against a child may occur for reasons other than a persistent

pedophilic interest. Full diagnostic criteria still require evidence of recurrent, intense arousal
pattern over time.

22. Which statement about transvestic disorder is most accurate?
   A. It is identical to gender dysphoria
   B. It refers to sexually arousing cross-dressing and may in some cases co-occur with gender

dysphoria
   C. It is a subtype of fetishistic disorder in all cases

 D. It is diagnosed whenever a person wears clothing associated with another gender
       Answer: B. It refers to sexually arousing cross-dressing and may in some cases co-occur
with gender dysphoria
   Rationale: Transvestic disorder involves sexually arousing cross-dressing. In some
individuals it may coexist with clinically significant gender dysphoria.

23. Which statement best describes gender dysphoria?
   A. It is a sexual dysfunction involving lack of arousal
   B. It is a paraphilic disorder involving atypical sexual targets
   C. It is marked by incongruence between experienced gender and assigned gender, usually

accompanied by clinically significant distress or impairment

 D. It is synonymous with homosexuality
       Answer: C. It is marked by incongruence between experienced gender and assigned
gender, usually accompanied by clinically significant distress or impairment
   Rationale: Gender dysphoria centers on distressing incongruence between experienced
gender and assigned gender, not on sexual dysfunction or atypical sexual targets.

24. Which feature is central in gender dysphoria among adolescents and adults?
   A. Persistent visual hallucinations
   B. Incongruence between experienced gender and assigned gender
   C. Compulsive sexual behavior

 D. Exclusive fetishistic interest in clothing
       Answer: B. Incongruence between experienced gender and assigned gender
   Rationale: In adolescents and adults, the central diagnostic feature is incongruence between
experienced gender and assigned gender.

25. Which statement best explains why gender dysphoria is not classified as a sexual
dysfunction?

   A. Because it does not involve any distress
   B. Because it concerns gender incongruence rather than disturbance in sexual response
   C. Because it always begins in childhood
   D. Because it is always caused by family conflict
  Answer: B. Because it concerns gender incongruence rather than disturbance in sexual

response
   Rationale: Gender dysphoria is classified separately because its core issue is gender

incongruence and related distress, not impaired desire, arousal, orgasm, or pain response.

26. A child persistently insists on being another gender, prefers roles and play associated with
that gender, and becomes increasingly distressed when puberty approaches. Which diagnosis
is most consistent?

   A. Fetishistic disorder
   B. Gender dysphoria
   C. Voyeuristic disorder

 D. Delusional disorder
       Answer: B. Gender dysphoria
   Rationale: Persistent insistence on being another gender, marked cross-gender identification,
and increasing distress around anticipated puberty are classic indicators consistent with gender
dysphoria.

27. Which statement is most accurate regarding the course of gender dysphoria in children?
   A. It invariably persists into adulthood
   B. It always disappears before adolescence
   C. It may persist, desist, or recur later in life

 D. It is diagnosed only after age 18
       Answer: C. It may persist, desist, or recur later in life
   Rationale: The course of gender dysphoria in childhood is variable. Some cases persist,
some desist, and some may recur later.

28. Which factor may reduce distress associated with gender dysphoria?
   A. Punitive correction of gender nonconformity
   B. Supportive environments and knowledge that treatments exist to reduce incongruence
   C. Forced disclosure in school settings

 D. Exposure-based aversion procedures
       Answer: B. Supportive environments and knowledge that treatments exist to reduce
incongruence
   Rationale: Supportive environments can mitigate distress, and awareness of treatment
options may reduce suffering associated with gender incongruence.

29. Which condition may complicate the differential diagnosis of gender dysphoria because

   A. Autism spectrum disorder
   B. Panic disorder
   C. Substance intoxication

 D. Specific learning disorder
       Answer: A. Autism spectrum disorder
   Rationale: DSM notes that autism spectrum disorder may complicate diagnosis because rigid
and concrete thinking about gender roles may resemble or obscure co-occurring gender
dysphoria.

CHAPTER 13: Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Disorders

1. Which of the following is a required feature for a diagnosis of schizophrenia?
  A. At least one manic episode
  B. At least one month of active-phase symptoms within a disturbance lasting at least six

months
  C. Delusions occurring without any other psychotic symptoms

 D. Full return to premorbid functioning within one month
       Answer: B. At least one month of active-phase symptoms within a disturbance lasting at
least six months
  Rationale: Schizophrenia requires continuous signs of disturbance for at least 6 months,
including at least 1 month of active-phase symptoms such as delusions, hallucinations,
disorganized speech, grossly disorganized or catatonic behavior, or negative symptoms. This is
the core duration framework for schizophrenia.

2. Which symptom is classified as a negative symptom of schizophrenia?
  A. Delusion
  B. Hallucination
  C. Avolition

 D. Flight of ideas
       Answer: C. Avolition
  Rationale: Avolition is a negative symptom involving decreased motivation and reduced
self-initiated purposeful behavior. It is one of the most prominent negative symptoms in
schizophrenia.

3. A client presents with delusions, hallucinations, and disorganized speech for 3 months. There
is no clear evidence yet of a six-month total duration. Which diagnosis is most consistent with
the presentation?

  A. Brief Psychotic Disorder
  B. Schizophreniform Disorder
  C. Schizoaffective Disorder

 D. Delusional Disorder
       Answer: B. Schizophreniform Disorder
  Rationale: Schizophreniform disorder has the same symptomatic presentation as
schizophrenia, but its duration is at least 1 month and less than 6 months. A 3-month duration
fits this range.

4. Which feature best distinguishes delusional disorder from schizophrenia?
  A. Presence of psychosis
  B. Presence of mood symptoms
  C. Absence of the other characteristic active-phase symptoms of schizophrenia

 D. Onset in adulthood
       Answer: C. Absence of the other characteristic active-phase symptoms of schizophrenia
  Rationale: Delusional disorder is distinguished by persistent delusions without the other
characteristic active-phase symptoms of schizophrenia, such as prominent hallucinations,
disorganized speech, grossly disorganized or catatonic behavior, and negative symptoms.

5. A person has psychotic symptoms lasting 10 days, then returns fully to premorbid functioning.
Which diagnosis is most appropriate?

  A. Schizophrenia
  B. Schizophreniform Disorder
  C. Brief Psychotic Disorder

 D. Delusional Disorder
       Answer: C. Brief Psychotic Disorder
  Rationale: Brief psychotic disorder involves psychotic symptoms lasting at least 1 day but less
than 1 month, followed by eventual full return to premorbid functioning.

6. Schizoaffective disorder is diagnosed when:
  A. Mood symptoms are absent throughout the illness
  B. Mood symptoms occur only after psychosis has completely resolved
  C. A major mood episode is present for the majority of the total duration of the illness, and

there is also a period of psychosis without prominent mood symptoms

 D. Delusions are the only psychotic symptom present for at least one month
       Answer: C. A major mood episode is present for the majority of the total duration of the
illness, and there is also a period of psychosis without prominent mood symptoms
  Rationale: Schizoaffective disorder requires both a major mood episode and
schizophrenia-type psychotic symptoms, plus at least a 2-week period of delusions or
hallucinations without prominent mood symptoms. Mood episodes must occupy the majority of
the total illness duration.

7. Which statement best differentiates schizophrenia from major depressive disorder with
psychotic features?

  A. In schizophrenia, psychotic symptoms occur only during mood episodes
  B. In schizophrenia, psychotic symptoms may occur outside mood episodes, and mood
episodes, if present, occupy only a minority of the illness duration
  C. In schizophrenia, catatonia cannot occur

 D. In schizophrenia, functioning is always preserved
       Answer: B. In schizophrenia, psychotic symptoms may occur outside mood episodes, and
mood episodes, if present, occupy only a minority of the illness duration
  Rationale: In schizophrenia, psychotic symptoms are not confined to mood episodes. If mood
episodes occur, they occupy only a minority of the total duration of the illness. This helps
distinguish it from mood disorders with psychotic features and schizoaffective disorder.

8. Which of the following would most strongly suggest delusional disorder rather than
schizophrenia?

  A. Flat affect and avolition
  B. One month of nonbizarre delusions without prominent hallucinations, disorganized speech,
or negative symptoms

  C. Grossly disorganized behavior with persecutory delusions

 D. Auditory hallucinations with loose associations
       Answer: B. One month of nonbizarre delusions without prominent hallucinations,
disorganized speech, or negative symptoms
  Rationale: That presentation fits delusional disorder because the psychosis is limited to
delusions, and the broader active-phase symptoms of schizophrenia are absent.

9. Which of the following is most characteristic of avolition?
  A. Increased goal-directed behavior
  B. Decreased ability to recognize familiar faces
  C. Reduction in self-initiated purposeful activities

 D. Excessive worry about future events
       Answer: C. Reduction in self-initiated purposeful activities
  Rationale: Avolition specifically refers to decreased motivation and reduced engagement in
purposeful activity, such as work, self-care, or social tasks.

10. Which of the following is an example of diminished emotional expression?
   A. Euphoric mood during a stressful interview
   B. Reduced eye contact, flattened prosody, and decreased facial expressiveness
   C. Pressured speech and distractibility

 D. Repetitive checking behavior
       Answer: B. Reduced eye contact, flattened prosody, and decreased facial expressiveness
   Rationale: Diminished emotional expression includes reduced facial expression, eye contact,
prosody, and gestures that would normally add emotional tone to speech.

11. Which diagnosis should be considered first when psychotic symptoms appear after recent
substance use?

   A. Schizotypal Personality Disorder
   B. Substance/Medication-Induced Psychotic Disorder
   C. Delusional Disorder

 D. Schizoaffective Disorder
       Answer: B. Substance/Medication-Induced Psychotic Disorder
   Rationale: When psychotic symptoms arise after recent substance use, a substance-induced
cause must be ruled out first because substances can directly cause psychotic presentations.

   A. Schizophrenia

   B. At least 1 month but less than 6 months

1. Neurodevelopmental disorders are best described as conditions that:

       Answer: B. Usually begin in the developmental period and produce impairments in

sequentially, so disruption of early skills can impair later skill acquisition.

  A. Specific learning disorder

  Rationale: DSM-consistent descriptions of inattention in ADHD include poor sustained

1. A 79-year-old patient becomes disoriented over one day, cannot sustain attention during
interview, and appears lucid in the morning but markedly confused at night. Which diagnosis is
most strongly suggested?

  A. Major neurocognitive disorder due to Alzheimer's disease
  B. Delirium
  C. Mild neurocognitive disorder

 D. Intellectual disability
       Answer: B. Delirium
  Rationale: Delirium is characterized by a disturbance in attention and awareness that
develops over a short period, usually hours to a few days, and tends to fluctuate during the day.
The acute onset and waxing-waning course are key clues.

2. Which feature best distinguishes major neurocognitive disorder from mild neurocognitive
disorder?

  A. Presence of any memory complaint
  B. Cognitive decline caused only by medical illness
  C. Interference with independence in everyday activities

 D. Presence of behavioral disturbance
       Answer: C. Interference with independence in everyday activities
  Rationale: Major neurocognitive disorder involves cognitive deficits that interfere with
independence in everyday activities, such as managing finances or medications. Mild
neurocognitive disorder allows independence to remain intact, often with compensatory effort.

3. A patient shows gradual decline in memory and learning, followed by worsening language
difficulty, with no evidence of mixed etiology. Which diagnosis is most consistent with this
pattern?

  A. Major neurocognitive disorder due to Alzheimer's disease
  B. Delirium due to another medical condition
  C. Major vascular neurocognitive disorder

 D. Factitious disorder
       Answer: A. Major neurocognitive disorder due to Alzheimer's disease
  Rationale: A gradual, steadily progressive decline beginning with memory and learning and
extending to additional domains is classic for Alzheimer-type major neurocognitive disorder,
especially when mixed etiologies are not evident.

4. Which cognitive pattern is especially suggestive of vascular neurocognitive disorder?
  A. Prominent early decline in complex attention and frontal-executive functioning
  B. Early hyperorality with relative sparing of memory
  C. Fluctuating alertness with recurrent visual hallucinations

 D. Isolated decline in social cognition only
       Answer: A. Prominent early decline in complex attention and frontal-executive functioning
  Rationale: Vascular neurocognitive disorder is especially associated with decline in complex
attention, processing speed, and frontal-executive abilities, often in relation to cerebrovascular
events.

5. A 72-year-old presents with cognitive decline, recurrent well-formed visual hallucinations,
marked fluctuations in attention, and spontaneous parkinsonian features. Which diagnosis is
most likely?

  A. Major neurocognitive disorder due to Alzheimer's disease
  B. Major neurocognitive disorder with Lewy bodies
  C. Major neurocognitive disorder due to traumatic brain injury

 D. Delusional disorder
       Answer: B. Major neurocognitive disorder with Lewy bodies
  Rationale: Lewy body disease is strongly suggested by fluctuating cognition, recurrent
well-formed visual hallucinations, and spontaneous parkinsonism after cognitive decline begins.

6. Which combination is most characteristic of the behavioral variant of frontotemporal
neurocognitive disorder?

  A. Aphasia, apraxia, and agnosia only
  B. Hyperorality, disinhibition, apathy, and loss of empathy
  C. Fluctuating consciousness and fever

 D. Recurrent panic attacks with avoidance
       Answer: B. Hyperorality, disinhibition, apathy, and loss of empathy
  Rationale: Behavioral variant frontotemporal neurocognitive disorder commonly presents with
disinhibition, apathy or inertia, loss of sympathy or empathy, compulsive behavior, and
hyperorality or dietary change.

7. A patient develops cognitive decline immediately after a serious head injury with loss of
consciousness and posttraumatic amnesia, and the deficits persist beyond the acute phase.

  A. Major or mild neurocognitive disorder due to traumatic brain injury
  B. Major neurocognitive disorder due to Alzheimer's disease
  C. Delirium only

 D. Somatic symptom disorder
       Answer: A. Major or mild neurocognitive disorder due to traumatic brain injury
  Rationale: The diagnosis requires evidence of traumatic brain injury, such as loss of
consciousness or posttraumatic amnesia, with cognitive disorder presenting immediately after
injury or after recovery of consciousness and persisting beyond the acute phase.

8. Which statement about delirium is most accurate?
  A. It typically has an insidious onset and stable course
  B. It is diagnosed only when memory impairment is present
  C. It involves a disturbance in attention and awareness that develops over a short period
  D. It excludes the possibility of coexisting neurocognitive disorder
  Answer: C. It involves a disturbance in attention and awareness that develops over a short

period
  Rationale: This is the essential feature of delirium: acute onset of impaired attention and

awareness, typically fluctuating over hours to days.

       Answer: D. Stable progressive course over several years
   Rationale: A stable progressive multi-year course is least consistent with delirium and more
consistent with major neurocognitive disorders such as Alzheimer's disease.

34. A psychometrician lacks sufficient competence in a specialized geriatric cognitive instrument
but is the only available provider in an emergency setting. What is the best ethical course?

   A. Refuse all services regardless of urgency
   B. Proceed recklessly because any help is better than none
   C. Provide only carefully limited emergency assistance, seek consultation, and arrange
appropriate referral as soon as possible
   D. Diagnose independently beyond one's competence because of experience with younger

adults
       Answer: C. Provide only carefully limited emergency assistance, seek consultation, and
arrange appropriate referral as soon as possible

   Rationale: Ethical standards require practice within competence, but they also allow limited
emergency service so individuals are not deprived of needed care. The professional must
proceed cautiously, seek competence support, and discontinue once appropriate services are
available.

35. Which diagnosis should be considered when cognitive impairment in an older adult
improves significantly after successful treatment of a depressive episode?

   A. Major neurocognitive disorder definitely due to Alzheimer's disease
   B. Delirium due to vascular disease
   C. Major depressive disorder with cognitive symptoms rather than a primary neurocognitive

 D. Frontotemporal neurocognitive disorder
       Answer: C. Major depressive disorder with cognitive symptoms rather than a primary
neurocognitive disorder
   Rationale: When cognition improves significantly after successful treatment of depression, the
picture is more consistent with depressive cognitive impairment than a primary progressive
neurocognitive disorder.

1. Which principle best justifies the State's authority to hospitalize a person with severe mental
illness who is unable to provide for basic needs such as food and shelter?

  A. Police power
  B. Parens patriae
  C. Habeas corpus

 D. Due process
       Answer: B. Parens patriae
  Rationale: Parens patriae refers to the State's authority to act as a surrogate parent for
individuals who cannot act in their own best interests, such as persons with severe mental
illness who cannot secure basic necessities or recognize their need for treatment.

2. Which situation best illustrates the exercise of police power in mental health law?
  A. The State appoints a guardian for an abandoned child
  B. The court orders treatment because a person cannot care for basic needs
  C. The government restricts a dangerous individual to protect public safety

 D. A patient voluntarily seeks psychiatric admission
       Answer: C. The government restricts a dangerous individual to protect public safety
  Rationale: Police power is the State's authority to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the
public. In mental health law, it is invoked when a person poses a danger to others or to the
community.

3. A patient voluntarily enters a psychiatric facility for treatment. This type of admission is best
described as:

  A. Civil commitment
  B. Criminal commitment
  C. Voluntary admission

 D. Competency restoration
       Answer: C. Voluntary admission
  Rationale: Voluntary admission occurs when a person personally seeks treatment and agrees
to enter a facility. It differs from civil or criminal commitment because it is not compelled by court
or legal process.

4. In civil commitment proceedings, which condition is most directly related to grave disability?
  A. The person has unusual beliefs
  B. The person cannot obtain basic necessities for survival
  C. The person has committed a felony

 D. The person refuses all psychotherapy
       Answer: B. The person cannot obtain basic necessities for survival
  Rationale: Grave disability refers to substantial inability to provide for essential needs such as
food, clothing, shelter, or basic self-care, making intervention legally relevant in civil
commitment.
5. Which statement best reflects a key procedural safeguard in civil commitment?
  A. The patient may be committed solely on a relative's verbal request
  B. The patient has no right to legal representation during proceedings
  C. The patient must be informed and allowed to participate in the legal process

 D. The court may commit a patient without any evaluation
       Answer: C. The patient must be informed and allowed to participate in the legal process
  Rationale: Civil commitment must include due process protections. The person must be
notified, present when appropriate, represented, and given opportunities to challenge evidence
and obtain independent evaluation.

6. Which trend is most consistent with a rights-based approach to mental health law?
  A. Lowering standards for involuntary hospitalization
  B. Increasing protection of individual liberty in commitment procedures
  C. Removing the need for judicial review

 D. Treating all refusal of treatment as proof of incompetence
       Answer: B. Increasing protection of individual liberty in commitment procedures
  Rationale: A rights-based or liberal approach in mental health law emphasizes fairness, due
process, and protection of individual liberty against unwarranted involuntary hospitalization.

7. A court orders a person with severe mental illness to comply with treatment while remaining
in the community rather than being hospitalized. This arrangement is best described as:

  A. Forensic diversion
  B. Assisted outpatient treatment
  C. Conditional insanity acquittal

 D. Parole supervision
       Answer: B. Assisted outpatient treatment
  Rationale: Assisted outpatient treatment is a court-ordered community-based treatment
arrangement for individuals with severe mental illness who require mandated care but not
necessarily inpatient confinement.

8. Which issue is most central in debates on involuntary commitment?
  A. Whether all mental disorders are hereditary
  B. How to balance individual liberty and public safety
  C. Whether psychotherapy is superior to medication

 D. How to eliminate all diagnostic error
       Answer: B. How to balance individual liberty and public safety
  Rationale: The central tension in involuntary commitment is the balance between respecting
autonomy and liberty on one hand, and protecting the individual or public from harm on the
other.

9. Criminal commitment is primarily concerned with:
  A. Access to community mental health funding
  B. A person's legal status after behavior that violates criminal law
  C. School-based mental health screening

 D. Voluntary psychiatric consultation
       Answer: B. A person's legal status after behavior that violates criminal law
  Rationale: Criminal commitment concerns mental condition within criminal proceedings,
including responsibility, insanity, competence, and disposition after criminal conduct.

10. Which legal question is addressed by the insanity defense?
   A. Whether the accused is likely to respond to psychotherapy
   B. Whether the accused was criminally responsible at the time of the act
   C. Whether the accused can afford private counsel

 D. Whether the accused currently has depressive symptoms
       Answer: B. Whether the accused was criminally responsible at the time of the act
   Rationale: The insanity defense addresses whether mental illness so impaired the accused at
the time of the offense that criminal responsibility should be reduced or removed.

11. Which standard focuses on whether a person knew the nature of the act or knew that it was
wrong?

   A. Tarasoff standard
   B. M'Naghten rule
   C. Frye rule

 D. Exclusionary rule
       Answer: B. M'Naghten rule
   Rationale: The M'Naghten rule focuses on cognition: whether the person knew the nature and
quality of the act or knew it was wrong.

12. Which legal formulation broadened criminal nonresponsibility by emphasizing whether an
unlawful act was the product of mental disease or defect?

   A. Durham rule
   B. M'Naghten rule
   C. Best evidence rule

 D. Parens patriae rule
       Answer: A. Durham rule
   Rationale: The Durham rule broadened criminal nonresponsibility by asking whether the
unlawful act was the product of mental disease or defect.

13. Which standard includes both cognitive incapacity and inability to control behavior because
of mental illness?

   A. Police power standard
   B. American Law Institute standard
   C. Duty to protect standard

 D. Grave disability standard
       Answer: B. American Law Institute standard
   Rationale: The American Law Institute standard includes both cognitive and volitional
elements, recognizing lack of substantial capacity either to appreciate wrongfulness or to
conform conduct to law.

14. Which statement best differentiates insanity at the time of the offense from competence to
stand trial?

   A. Insanity concerns present ability to assist counsel, whereas competence concerns past
mental state

   B. Insanity and competence are identical determinations
   C. Insanity concerns mental state during the act, whereas competence concerns present
ability to participate in defense
   D. Insanity applies only in civil cases, whereas competence applies only in correctional

settings
       Answer: C. Insanity concerns mental state during the act, whereas competence concerns
present ability to participate in defense

   Rationale: Insanity is retrospective and evaluates the accused at the time of the offense.
Competence to stand trial is present-focused and asks whether the defendant can understand
proceedings and assist counsel.

15. A defendant understands the charges but cannot communicate rationally with counsel
because of severe psychosis. The most appropriate legal concern is:

   A. Diminished capacity
   B. Competence to stand trial
   C. Parens patriae

 D. Right to treatment
       Answer: B. Competence to stand trial
   Rationale: A defendant must be able to understand proceedings and assist counsel rationally.
Severe psychosis interfering with communication raises competence concerns.

16. Which statement best describes diminished capacity?
   A. It concerns whether treatment is available in a public hospital
   B. It refers to reduced ability to form the required criminal intent because of mental

   C. It is identical to voluntary admission

 D. It automatically leads to full acquittal
       Answer: B. It refers to reduced ability to form the required criminal intent because of
mental disturbance
   Rationale: Diminished capacity refers to impaired mental functioning that may reduce the
ability to form specific intent necessary for certain crimes.

17. A therapist learns that a client has made a serious, specific threat to kill an identified former

   B. Warn or protect the identifiable potential victim and take appropriate steps

       Answer: B. Warn or protect the identifiable potential victim and take appropriate steps
   Rationale: When a client makes a serious, specific threat toward an identifiable person, legal
and ethical principles may require breaching confidentiality to protect the threatened individual
and reduce imminent risk.

18. Which condition most strongly supports activation of the duty to warn or protect?
   A. The client vaguely says, "People annoy me"
   B. The client reports old anger with no plan or target
   C. The client makes a specific threat toward an identifiable person

 D. The client refuses to discuss childhood experiences
       Answer: C. The client makes a specific threat toward an identifiable person
   Rationale: Duty to warn or protect is strongest when there is a specific threat against an
identifiable victim, as opposed to vague or nonspecific hostility.

19. Why is the duty to warn ethically complex?
   A. It eliminates the need for informed consent
   B. It may require breaching confidentiality to prevent serious harm
   C. It prohibits consultation with colleagues

 D. It applies only when a court order already exists
       Answer: B. It may require breaching confidentiality to prevent serious harm
   Rationale: The ethical tension lies in competing duties: protect client confidentiality while also
protecting potential victims or the public from serious foreseeable harm.

20. Mental health professionals serving as expert witnesses are primarily expected to:

   B. Provide specialized, objective knowledge to assist the court
   C. Replace the judge's legal reasoning
