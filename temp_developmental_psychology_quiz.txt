Question 1:
A developmental psychologist studies how people change from conception to late adulthood
while also examining which characteristics remain relatively stable. This best reflects which
definition?

Choices:
A. Abnormal psychology
B. Human development
C. Evolutionary biology
D. Educational assessment

Correct Answer:
B. Human development

Rationale:

Correct Answer Explanation:
B is correct because human development is the scientific study of systematic processes of
change and stability across the life span.

Incorrect Answers Explanation:
A. Abnormal psychology focuses on maladaptive behavior and psychopathology, not the full life
span study of change and stability.
B. This is correct because it captures both change and stability from conception onward.
C. Evolutionary biology focuses on species-level adaptation, not the full developmental study of
individual life-span change.
D. Educational assessment focuses on measuring learning or performance, not overall
life-span development.

High-Yield Concept: Human development includes both change and stability.

1. A 4-year-old describes herself as "fast," "pretty," and "the one with the red shoes," but cannot

B. Severe functional impairment and need for daily assistance

B. Aging in place

1. Death is best understood as which of the following?
  A. A purely biological event with little social relevance
  B. A phenomenon with biological, social, cultural, legal, psychological, and ethical dimensions
  C. A medical failure that should always be prevented

 D. A uniform experience interpreted similarly across cultures
       Answer: B. A phenomenon with biological, social, cultural, legal, psychological, and ethical
dimensions
  Rationale: Death is not merely a biological event. It also has social, cultural, historical,
religious, legal, psychological, developmental, medical, and ethical meanings, making this the
most complete and accurate answer.

2. Which statement best describes the mortality revolution?
  A. Death became more common in early childhood than in late adulthood
  B. Infectious diseases replaced chronic diseases as the leading causes of death
  C. Death rates dropped markedly, and death became concentrated more in later life

 D. Funeral rituals disappeared as life expectancy increased
       Answer: C. Death rates dropped markedly, and death became concentrated more in later
life
  Rationale: The mortality revolution refers to the historical decline in death rates, especially in
developed societies, which shifted death from a common early-life event to one more
concentrated in late adulthood.

3. In many modern societies, death became less visible primarily because:
  A. people stopped grieving for loved ones
  B. death increasingly occurred in institutions and under professional care
  C. religion completely lost influence on mourning practices

 D. families became unwilling to discuss illness of any kind
       Answer: B. death increasingly occurred in institutions and under professional care
  Rationale: Death became less visible in modern societies because many dying persons were
cared for in hospitals and other institutions, making death more removed from ordinary family
life.

4. The field that systematically studies death and dying is known as:
  A. Gerontology
  B. Thanatology
  C. Nosology

 D. Ethology
       Answer: B. Thanatology
  Rationale: Thanatology is the systematic study of death and dying and is directly associated
with efforts to understand end-of-life processes more realistically and compassionately.
5. Hospice care is best described as:
  A. aggressive curative treatment for terminal illness
  B. patient- and family-centered care for a person with a terminal illness
  C. a legal process for refusing treatment

 D. hospitalization intended to prolong life indefinitely
       Answer: B. patient- and family-centered care for a person with a terminal illness
  Rationale: Hospice care focuses on compassionate, patient- and family-centered support for a
terminally ill person, emphasizing comfort, dignity, and quality of life.

6. Palliative care primarily aims to:
  A. cure terminal illness through experimental treatment
  B. relieve pain and suffering while preserving comfort and dignity
  C. determine whether a client is mentally competent

 D. delay grief reactions among family members
       Answer: B. relieve pain and suffering while preserving comfort and dignity
  Rationale: Palliative care aims to reduce suffering and improve comfort and dignity, not
necessarily to cure disease. It is central to humane end-of-life care.

7. Which statement best differentiates hospice care from curative care?
  A. Hospice care focuses on quality of life rather than reversing disease
  B. Hospice care is only for patients in intensive care units
  C. Hospice care excludes family participation

 D. Hospice care is identical to emergency psychiatric intervention
       Answer: A. Hospice care focuses on quality of life rather than reversing disease
  Rationale: Hospice care prioritizes comfort, dignity, and quality of life for people unlikely to
benefit from curative medical intervention.

8. Terminal drop refers to:
  A. the decline in religious faith near death
  B. a sudden refusal of food due to depression alone
  C. a frequently observed decline in cognitive abilities shortly before death

 D. a decrease in social support after bereavement
       Answer: C. a frequently observed decline in cognitive abilities shortly before death
  Rationale: Terminal drop refers to cognitive decline often observed before death, even when
demographic and health factors are considered.

9. Which of the following is most consistent with a near-death experience?
  A. Increased appetite and improved memory
  B. A sense of leaving the body or moving through a tunnel toward light
  C. Immediate acceptance of death without distress

 D. Complete loss of consciousness with no later recall
       Answer: B. A sense of leaving the body or moving through a tunnel toward light
  Rationale: Near-death experiences commonly include reports of being out of the body, moving
through a tunnel, or encountering bright lights or mystical imagery.
10. Kübler-Ross proposed that many dying persons may experience:
   A. repression, projection, displacement, and sublimation
   B. denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance
   C. shock, denial, panic, regression, and recovery

 D. despair, dependency, hostility, guilt, and detachment
       Answer: B. denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance
   Rationale: Kübler-Ross proposed these five commonly cited reactions in confronting death.

11. A major criticism of Kübler-Ross's stage model is that:
   A. dying persons never experience anger
   B. grief reactions are always identical across persons
   C. not everyone passes through all stages or in the same order

 D. acceptance is always the first stage in terminal illness
       Answer: C. not everyone passes through all stages or in the same order
   Rationale: A major criticism is that the stage sequence is not universal. People may skip
stages, repeat them, or experience them in varying order.

12. Terror management theory suggests that awareness of death often leads people to:
   A. abandon all cultural values
   B. become more committed to meaningful worldviews and relationships
   C. lose the capacity for attachment

 D. reject self-esteem as irrelevant
       Answer: B. become more committed to meaningful worldviews and relationships
   Rationale: Terror management theory proposes that awareness of mortality can intensify
commitment to cultural worldviews, self-esteem, and attachment-related bonds that provide
meaning and psychological security.

13. Grief is best defined as:
   A. the legal disposition of the dead person's property
   B. the emotional response that generally follows closely on the heels of death
   C. a psychiatric disorder in all bereaved persons

 D. the formal ritual conducted during burial
       Answer: B. the emotional response that generally follows closely on the heels of death
   Rationale: Grief refers to the emotional response to death or loss, especially the immediate
emotional reaction.

14. Bereavement is best understood as:
   A. only the moment of hearing that someone has died

       Answer: B. an emotional process of adjustment to the loss of a significant person

15. Which statement best reflects contemporary research on grieving?
   A. There is one universal normal sequence of mourning

   C. Grieving varies widely, and resilience is common

 D. Intense distress must always continue for many years
       Answer: C. Grieving varies widely, and resilience is common
   Rationale: Contemporary research rejects a single universal grief pattern and recognizes

16. Which pattern of grieving has been widely studied?

   C. Bargaining, hostility, amnesia, then resignation

       Answer: A. Shock and disbelief, preoccupation with the deceased, then resolution
   Rationale: One widely studied pattern of grief moves from initial shock and disbelief to
preoccupation with the deceased and later toward resolution or adjustment.

   A. reversible and temporary
   B. punishment for misbehavior
   C. final and universal

 D. only relevant to older adults
       Answer: C. final and universal
   Rationale: A mature understanding of death includes recognizing irreversibility and

18. Which statement about children's understanding of death is most accurate?

   B. Understanding develops gradually with cognitive and emotional maturation
   C. Children do not grieve because they cannot understand death

       Answer: B. Understanding develops gradually with cognitive and emotional maturation
   Rationale: Children's understanding of death changes with developmental growth. Their grief

   D. absence of grief after peer death

   C. tends to increase

       Answer: C. tends to increase

   B. role change plus possible social and economic consequences
   C. immediate recovery due to social support alone

       Answer: B. role change plus possible social and economic consequences
   Rationale: Widowhood often requires adjustment not only to emotional loss but also to new

   C. It is always less distressing than job loss

       Answer: B. It may precipitate changes in self-concept and relationships with others
   Rationale: Loss of a parent in adulthood can still reshape self-understanding, family roles,

       Answer: B. such loss is often minimized, leaving parents with limited social support
   Rationale: Miscarriage is often not publicly recognized to the same degree as other losses,

       Answer: C. Isolation, depression, conflict, financial stress, and debilitating illness
